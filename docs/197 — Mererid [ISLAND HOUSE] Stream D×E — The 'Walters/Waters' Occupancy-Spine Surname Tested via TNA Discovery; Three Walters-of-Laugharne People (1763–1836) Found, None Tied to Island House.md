## 3. The finding

**The occupancy-spine "Walters → Island House" association is UNSOURCED from TNA Discovery.** Reporting that honestly is the point of the pass, not a failure — the same bounded-negative outcome as White (Doc 195) and Prydderch (Doc 193).

What the sweep *does* establish is a modest but real cluster: **three genuine Walters/Waters people "of Laugharne" across 1763–1836**, none tied to a house at object level:

1. **Elizabeth Walters**, a married woman of Laugharne, will proved 1763.
2. **Jane Walters**, a widow of Laugharne, will proved 1829.
3. **Thomas Waters, esq**, formerly of Laugharne and by 1836 of St Albans — a man of enough property to warrant a Chancery lunacy commission.

Two wills sixty-six years apart and one propertied esquire show the name present in the town across three generations' span, but the catalogue puts **no house** against any of them, still less Island House. Whatever placed "Walters" on the Island House occupancy spine, it is **not** these records.

**A spelling caveat held open, not closed.** "Waters" (Thomas the esquire) is a plausible orthographic variant of "Walters" (the two testatrixes) — the dropped medial *l* is a common Welsh-record variant — but the catalogue spells them differently and the objects share no named link. Their identity as one family is therefore **NOT asserted**; it is a variant candidate to be settled only by a body read, not by the spelling resemblance.

### The collisions caught (method value)

One surname sweep produced three distinct, well-worn collision types, each of which could have entered the record as a false "Walters of Island House":

- **Subject-word "water."** The single most productive false-friend this pass: water *mills*, water-*corn grist mills*, *low* water (tide), and a "Corporation Water" bill. None is the surname.
- **Forename "Walter."** Walter Devereux, Sir Walter Raleigh, Sir Walter Montague, Walter Lloyd, Walter Rutter, Walter Dobson — and the OCR reading "Water Bush" for a forename Walter. All Christian names, not the surname.
- **Surname "Laugharne."** Rear-Admiral **John Laugharne** fills the Admiralty files — the town-as-surname trap Doc 191 caught with Rowland Laugharne.

Co-occurrence on a page or an item is not evidence; only the two Walters wills and the one Waters lunacy file put a **Walters/Waters** in the same clause as the **town** of Laugharne, and none of the three puts one in a **house**.

### The OCR-variant lesson, demonstrated live

The pass is worth logging chiefly for one methodological result: **the "Waters" spelling caught a person the "Walters" spelling could not see.** Thomas Waters, esq — the most substantial figure of the three — appears on *Waters Laugharne* and is absent from *Walters Laugharne*. Had the sweep stopped at the head spelling, as an unwary run would, the propertied esquire would have been missed entirely. This is exactly the OCR/orthographic-variant blindness named in the Doc 168/181 method lineage, caught here in the act. "Watters" then returned zero — the variant set is bounded, and now recorded.
