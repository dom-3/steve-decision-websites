One story, four customers. The thread through all of them is provenance and place — grown, baked, reared and poured in Laugharne. Each walks through a different door, but they're buying the same thing: something real, from here, done properly.
MH
The regulars

Menna & Dai Hughes

Late 50s–60s · Laugharne
Empty-nesters, comfortable
Frequent, steady spend
Their world

Locals for decades; the town's still home. They quietly want somewhere they're proud to bring visiting friends — without driving to Carmarthen for it.

Their week

A coffee-and-a-loaf a few mornings; Friday early drinks; a proper dinner once or twice a month; Sunday with family.

What they want

To be known by name. Consistency over novelty. A place that feels like theirs, and to back something local and good.

How we fit

The daily bakery habit, the butcher and deli for home, the wine bar as their regular. Part of their week, not an occasion.

The message that lands
"Your local — done properly."
RD
The local that travels

Rhian Davies

Early 40s · Narberth
Professional, dual-income
High spend on experiences
Their world

Busy career, money but little time. Follows food accounts, reads the Good Food Guide, "buys less but better," cares where food comes from and about a serious wine list.

Their week

Flat-out Mon–Fri; Friday/Saturday night is sacred — a booked dinner with a story. Always hunting the next place to discover and recommend.

What they want

A destination night worth a 30-minute drive. Discovery, provenance, an excellent wine list, food that tastes as good as it looks.

How we fit

The evening kitchen and wine bar are her sweet spot — the wine-led, seasonal, local table nobody near her offers.

The message that lands
"Worth the drive — Laugharne's wine & seasonal table."
PF
The coast escapers

The Preston family

Mid-40s + two kids · from Bristol
Second home / long lets
Free-spending, holiday mode
Their world

Own or rent on the Pembrokeshire coast, escaping the city for "authentic" west Wales. On holiday the priority is the best local experiences — "where do the locals actually eat?"

Their week (on holiday)

Slow mornings, a bakery run with the kids, beach or castle days, local produce for the cottage, dinners out at the recommended spots. They post it all.

What they want

The real Laugharne — authentic, local, organic, a story to take home. Somewhere that makes the trip feel special.

How we fit

Every door — bakery mornings, butcher & deli for the cottage, the kitchen for the standout dinner. Their basecamp for the stay.

The message that lands
"The real Laugharne table — grown, baked and poured here."
DT
The passing trade

The day-trippers

30s–70s · here for the day
Dylan Thomas, castle, estuary
Moderate, impulse spend
Their world

Here for the heritage — a day out, not a local. They don't know the town; they eat wherever looks charming and rates well on Google.

Their week

A day-trip, not a habit — so the job is one great impression that turns them into "we must come back for dinner" and a five-star review.

What they want

A charming daytime stop — proper coffee, fresh bread and cake, a light lunch with a view, a warm welcome.

How we fit

Coffee shop and bakery catch them by day; a light lunch upsells; the provenance story plants the seed to return.

The message that lands
"The heart of Laugharne — coffee, fresh bread, a proper welcome."

For the kitchen & bar — design to suit them

Emyr (kitchen) and Osian (bar): every special and every Cocktail of the Week is designed for these four. Keep a familiar comfort the regulars will return for; give the traveller something to discover and a wine worth the drive; make it authentic, organic and story-rich for the escapers; and give the day-tripper a charming, memorable first taste. One story — provenance and place — cooked and poured to suit whoever's walking in.
