The Globe Bakery — The Plan

A premium organic bakery that leads with one thing — selling great bread. Simple, focused, Welsh. We start from the bread itself, price it, and build a clean bread-first website around it (patisserie comes later). Alex Gooch is our reference. First of the three to go live.

Already settled

Name The Globe Bakery · subscription loaf + pastry box · bread is the hero (patisserie later) · look & feel: simple, organic, Welsh — Alex Gooch is our reference.

Design direction — from the walkthrough with Steve

What we saw looking at the site together — the north star for the build.

  • Lead with selling the bread. Straight to it — the old direction buried it and drifted toward coffee. Bread is what we sell.
  • Keep it simple. The site mustn't feel overcomplicated — focused, clean, easy to buy.
  • Showcase the bread. Photography-led, the loaves front and centre.
  • Organic + Welsh feel — Alex Gooch is our reference. The best site we saw: it showcases the bread and matches on organic, bakery and Welsh. We aim for that.
  • Show the price clearly. No hunting for it.
  • No hero image bleeding past the screen. Steve doesn't like the hero shooting past the background — keep it contained and tidy.
  • Bread is the hero; a few loaf types. Patisserie can come in later, but it is not the focus.

What we need you to direct, Steve

The name and the box are decided — these are the facts and calls that unlock the build.

What do you sell, at what price — and how many can you make a week?
Sets the shop catalogue, the subscription price, and the cap so we never oversell what the ovens can make.
How do people order from you today?
Tells us what we're replacing/upgrading and what habits customers already have.
Can you deliver — own driver, courier, or collection only? What radius?
Decides the fulfilment logic on the site and how far the subscription can reach.
What's the one product people already come to you for?
That's the hero we lead the brand and the launch with.
How does the wine bar relate to the bakery?
Decides whether we build one brand, two linked brands, or keep them fully separate — it changes the site and the branding.
What does success look like 12 months from now?
Sets the target we build and report against.
Rough budget for getting the bakery live?
Lets Rhys put real costs + payback against each part of the build.

Questions for the bakery-team meeting

Take these into the sit-down with the bakery team — the answers become the skeleton (the bread + the price) we build the website and business around. Fill them in here and it's tracked.

Which loaf (or few loaves) do we lead with?
The hero product the whole site is built around.
What can we make consistently, day to day?
We only put on the site what the ovens can deliver reliably.
What's the price per loaf (and any premium line)?
Sets the shop price and the subscription maths.
How many loaves can we produce a day / week?
Caps the shop + subscription so we never oversell.
Which days do we bake, and what are the order cut-offs / lead times?
Drives the collection + delivery logic on the site.
Are we certified organic? Welsh / local flour? The provenance story?
This is the premium + Welsh story the brand leans on.
Shelf life and packaging — good for collection and local delivery?
Decides how far the subscription can travel and how it's boxed.
Which bakes look best to showcase?
We build the photo-led site around the most beautiful bakes.
Anything people already ask for? Any spare capacity for wholesale (to supply Island House)?
Bestsellers guide the launch; wholesale is a cross-business link.
