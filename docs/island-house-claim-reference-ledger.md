# Island House, Laugharne — Sourced Reference Ledger

**Purpose:** a book-grade, claim-by-claim ledger for "the book of Island House." Every factual claim published on the owners' site (islandhouse.wales — chiefly the home page, incl. the Mary Curtis 1880 passage, and the "Owners and People 1778–2020" page) is given a verdict, the exact source citation(s) from our research register, and — for anything discredited — a reason and counter-source.

**Sources of citations (only two legitimate wells):** (1) the Airtable *Research — Sources* table (base `appnt9vSQKrKyaKiZ`); (2) sources explicitly named in our knowledge docs. Where a website claim has no matching fact/source in our library it is marked **UNSOURCED IN OUR LIBRARY — to research**. No citation, shelfmark, author or date below is invented; source records are quoted as they read in the register.

**Tier key (register's own):** T1 primary · T2 secondary · T3 tertiary/signpost. **Status key (Facts table):** Verified-strong / Verified / Referenced-pending / Tradition / Refuted.

**Compiled:** 23 Jul 2026 · from *Research — Facts* (tbloFM9ZTeT4LeSVQ) and *Research — Sources* (tblN0BdtHZwnS5OEa).

- **Register facts:**
  - "F221-2 — Tradition (Curtis 1880): in the Island House garden 'the foundation of a rounded tower was discovered underground in 1818; also Roman baths and leaden pipes; an Etruscan urn was found in the Roman remains.'" (Tradition)

## (C) THEIR CLAIMS WE REFUTE (reason + counter-source)

### C1 — "a **16th century** … house"
- **Verdict:** **REFUTED / superseded.** The standing fabric is older: roof timbers dated **1437**.
- **Counter (register fact):** "Island House's roof timbers are dated to 1437 (dendrochronology + radiocarbon)." (Verified) — *note:* the timbers pre-date the C16–C17 standing house; reuse/earlier phase remains open.
- **Counter-source:** Heneb — 'Medieval quay… first found in Wales' — Heneb (Ken Murphy) — 2025 — Ref *heneb.org.uk/medieval-quay-discovered…* — **T1**.
- **Why:** a hard scientific date (1437) places 15th-century fabric a century earlier than the "16th century" style-guess.

### C2 — "the cannon-balls fired on the castle by **Cromwell's** soldiers" / "Cromwell's troops … 1644"
- **Verdict:** **REFUTED (the "Cromwell" attribution).** Cromwell was not personally present; the 1644 siege of the Royalist-held castle was by Parliamentary forces under **Major-General Rowland Laugharne**, lasting about a week at the end of October 1644 (28 Oct–3 Nov).
- **Counter (register facts):**
  - "The castle was 'partly destroyed in the Parliamentarian siege of 1644 under General Rowland Laugharne'." (Verified)
  - "REFUTED AS WRITTEN — Lewis names the parliamentarian commander 'Lieutenant-General William Laugharne' and says the castle was 'besieged by Cromwell'" — logged as a source-error (commander was *Rowland*, not William; Cromwell not present). (Refuted)
  - "SAVE's 'damaged by cannon fire by Cromwell's troops … 1644' is unsupported by the statutory record: the Cadw listing names neither Cromwell nor cannon, and records only Mary Curtis' conjecture that the house once extended in a longer front range 'destroyed in the siege of the castle during the Civil War'." (Refuted)
- **Counter-sources:** Cadw, Full Report for Listed Buildings, ref. 9653 — Laugharne Castle — Cadw — Ref *Cadw 9653; Grade I* — **T2**. · Samuel Lewis, *A Topographical Dictionary of Wales* (4th edn, 1849), entry 'LAUGHARNE' — Samuel Lewis — 1849 — **T2** (cited to show even Lewis mis-stated it). · Cadw statutory listing — Island House (ref 9671) — **T1 primary** (names neither Cromwell nor cannon). · Supporting context: BCW Project — 'Civil War in South Wales, 1644-5', and Castles, Forts and Battles — 'Laugharne Castle' (siege enabled by Gerard's Oct 1644 withdrawal) — **T2**.
- **Keep for the book:** the damage-in-the-siege *legend* may be told (B2), but the agent must be Rowland Laugharne's Parliamentarians, not "Cromwell."

### C3 — Related lore corrected: "Van Dyck and Cromwell stayed at Gosport House, Cromwell to watch the siege"
- **Verdict:** **TRADITION, anachronistic** (a Laugharne Civil-War myth in circulation; not an Island House claim, logged so it is not laundered in).
- **Register fact:** "Local tradition, recorded in the listing, holds that Van Dyck and Cromwell stayed at Gosport House, Cromwell to watch the siege of the castle." (Tradition) — anachronistic: the 1644 siege was Rowland Laugharne's; Cromwell's SW-Wales campaign was 1648 (Pembroke); Van Dyck died 1641.
- **Source:** Cadw statutory listing — Gosport House, Laugharne Township — Cadw — **T2/T3 (listing tradition)**.

### C4 — "the Mansels [of Margam] were lords of Laugharne" (a common regional myth)
- **Verdict:** **REFUTED.** Mansel of Margam was a Glamorgan dynasty with no Laugharne interest; the real Mansel link is maternal/marital via the **Trimsaran** branch.
- **Counter (register facts):** "Mansel of Margam had no Laugharne interest (a Glamorgan dynasty)." (Refuted) · "The Mansel connection to Laugharne is MATERNAL/MARITAL, not seigneurial: the Trimsaran branch entered Laugharne through the marriage of Mary Mansel to John Hughes…" (Verified).
- **Counter-sources:** DWB — Mansel of Oxwich, Penrice & Margam — Ref *biography.wales/article/s-MANS-OXW-1250* — **T2**. · thePeerage.com — Mansel of Trimsaran pedigree (after Burke's Peerage 2003 / Cokayne) — Ref *thepeerage.com/p39906.htm* — **T2**.

### C5 — "Sir Sackville Crowe sold Laugharne to Sir William Russell"
- **Verdict:** **REFUTED as a land sale** (per our knowledge-doc finding: a *naval* succession — Treasurer of the Navy — misread as a land sale). **No dedicated fact record for the Crowe→Russell "sale" surfaced in the register on this pass**; what the register *does* verify about Crowe supports the correction.
- **What the register verifies about Crowe:** "Sackville Crowe secured a reversionary lease of the former Perrot lordship of Laugharne from Prince Charles early 1617; the grant fell in on the death of the Countess of Northumberland, Apr 1619." (Verified) · "HoP describes Crowe's Laugharne interest as the 'lordship of Laugharne'; it does NOT state that he occupied the castle / Perrot mansion." (Referenced-pending).
- **Source:** History of Parliament Online — CROWE, Sackville (1595–1671), of Laugharne, Carm. — Alan Davidson & Andrew Thrush — *The Commons 1604–1629* (pub. 2010) — **T2 secondary**.
- **Flag:** the specific "sold to Russell = false / it was the naval treasurership" debunk should be re-confirmed to a dedicated source before the book prints it (see E).
