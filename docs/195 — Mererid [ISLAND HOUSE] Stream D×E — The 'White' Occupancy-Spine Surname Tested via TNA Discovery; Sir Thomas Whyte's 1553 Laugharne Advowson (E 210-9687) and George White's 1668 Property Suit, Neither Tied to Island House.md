## 2. What was read (object level)

TNA Discovery, `discovery.nationalarchives.gov.uk`, read direct via the JS-capable browser. Every catalogue description below was opened at its own `/details/r/…` page and read in full; the results-page snippets were **not** trusted. Three spellings were run: **White**, **Whyte**, **Wight**.

### 2a. `White Laugharne` → 8 records; only 3 concern the TOWN

All three town records are Court of Chancery, Six Clerks Office pleadings, undigitised:

- **C 8/321/274 — *White v Ewens* (bill), 1668** (`/details/r/C5470681`). Plaintiff: **George White**. Defendants: John Ewens, Anne Ewens his wife, William Rutland and William Jones. Subject: **"property in Laugharne, Carmarthenshire."** Document type: bill only.
- **C 8/182/291 — *White v Ewens* (answer), 1668** (`/details/r/C5469328`). Plaintiff: George White. Defendants: as above **plus Alexander Brett**. Subject: **"property in Whitland, Laugharne etc, Carmarthenshire."** Answer only.
- **C 5/450/22 — *Brett v Ewens* (bill), 1672** (`/details/r/C3914947`). Plaintiffs: **Sir Edward Brett, kt, Richard Brett and Elizabeth Brett, widow.** Defendants: John Ewens, Anne Ewens his wife, and others. Subject: **"property in Laugharne, White Staunton etc, Carmarthenshire, Somerset, etc."** Bill only.

The other **five** `White Laugharne` hits are collisions, not the town (see §3): a naval victualling letter (ADM 106/675/309, 1712), two Captains'-letters files naming "Rear Admiral **of the White**" (ADM 1/1553/170 and ADM 1/1859/72, 1812), a merchant-navy crew list (BT 99/3165/24, 1915), and a Navy medical journal (ADM 101/266/1, 1871).

### 2b. `Whyte Laugharne` → 1 record; genuine and earlier

- **E 210/9687 — Ancient Deed, Series D, 1 Mary (19 Jul 1553 – 5 Jul 1554).** "John Bendisshe, gentleman, to **Sir Thomas Whyte, knight**, James Nycholas, clerk, and David Palmer and Henry Palmer, yeoman: Grant of his interest in the … **presentation to the rectory of the parish church of Laugharne (Talagharne): Carmarthenshire**." An **advowson** — the right of presentation to a church living — not a house or residence.

### 2c. `Wight Laugharne` → 3 records; all collisions

All three are Admiralty Captains'-letters files (ADM 1/2342/35, ADM 1/2346/137, ADM 1/1859/72, 1812–14) in which "**Wight**" is the **Isle of Wight** the convoys passed and "**Laugharne**" is **Rear Admiral John Laugharne**'s surname. Zero relevance to the surname White or the town.

## 3. The finding

**The occupancy-spine "White → Island House" association is UNSOURCED from TNA Discovery.** Reporting that honestly is the point of the pass, not a failure — the same outcome as Prydderch (Doc 193).

Two genuine White/Whyte × town-of-Laugharne threads do exist, and both are real people connected to Laugharne — but each is connected to **property or a church living, never to the house**:

1. **Sir Thomas Whyte, kt (1553–54)** holds the interest in the **advowson of Laugharne rectory** — ecclesiastical patronage, not occupancy. Whether this is the London merchant and Lord Mayor Sir Thomas White (d. 1567), founder of St John's College Oxford, who acquired advowsons across the country, is **not established from the object** and is deliberately left unasserted here.
2. **George White (1668)** is a plaintiff disputing **"property in Laugharne"** (and Whitland) against the **Ewens** family; the quarrel runs on into **Brett v Ewens (1672)** over the same Laugharne land. He is the earliest documentary George White with a Laugharne property interest — but the object names **no house**, still less Island House.

So the earliest White/Whyte figures in the Laugharne field are a churchman-patron and a land litigant. Whatever put "White" at the head of the Island House occupancy spine, it was **not** these records.

### The collisions caught (method value)

One surname sweep produced **three distinct collision types**, each of which could have entered the record as a false "White of Island House":

- **Surname "Laugharne."** Rear Admiral **John Laugharne** and **William Laugharne** fill the Admiralty records (ADM 106/675/309; ADM 1/1553/170; ADM 1/1859/72; ADM 101/266/1). "Laugharne" here is a family name, not the town — the same trap Doc 191 caught with Rowland Laugharne.
- **Naval "White."** "Rear Admiral **of the White**" is the Royal Navy's **White Squadron**, a rank-of-squadron, not the surname White.
- **Place "White / Wight."** "**White Staunton**" is **Whitestaunton, Somerset** (the Bretts' Somerset property); the "**Wight**" hits are the **Isle of Wight**. Both are place-names.

A fourth, softer guard: "**Whitland**" (a town near Laugharne) sits beside "White" in the 1668 answer, but the catalogue keeps them distinct — Whitland the place, White the plaintiff — so no conflation was adopted.

Co-occurrence on an item or a page is not evidence; only the three Chancery pleadings and the one Exchequer deed put a **White/Whyte** in the same clause as the **town** of Laugharne, and none of the four puts one in the **house**.
