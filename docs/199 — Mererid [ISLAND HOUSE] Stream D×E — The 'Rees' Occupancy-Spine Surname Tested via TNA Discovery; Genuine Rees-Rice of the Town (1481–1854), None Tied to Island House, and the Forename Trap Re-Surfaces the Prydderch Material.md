## 2. What was read (object level)

TNA Discovery, `discovery.nationalarchives.gov.uk`, read direct via the JS-capable browser. Three spellings were run — **Rees** (34 records), **Rice** (14), **Rhys** (1) — each filtered to The National Archives holdings. ### 2a. `Rees Laugharne` → 34 records; the genuine surname-Rees *of the town*

Four Prerogative Court of Canterbury wills name a **Rees** *of Laugharne* as testator, and two Chancery suits name a surname-Rees party at Laugharne:

- **PROB 11/1742/74 — Will of *Richard Rees of Laugharne*, Carmarthenshire, proved 6 June 1828** (register Sutton, quire 351–400). Confirmed at object level, detail page D185624.
- **PROB 11/1180/104 — Will of *David Rees of Laugharne*, proved 10 June 1789** (register Macham, quire 296–342). The earliest surname-Rees testator of the town in the sweep.
- **PROB 11/1958/222 — Will of *Rosamond Rees, widow, of Laugharne*, proved 5 Feb 1842** (vol. 3, quire 101–150).
- **C 11/2320/14 — *Jones v Rees*, depositions taken at Laugharne, bill 1742.** Plaintiff William Jones, gent; defendants **John Rees** and Rees Jones. (The co-defendant "Rees Jones" already shows *Rees as a forename* inside the very same record.)
- **C 13/376/3 — *Rees v Edwardes*, bill and two answers, 1837** (with depositions at **C 13/567/9**). Plaintiff **Richard Rees**; defendants **Sir William Philipps Laugharne Philipps** and William Edwardes. See the near-collision note in §3 — the "Laugharne" the search matched here is the *defendant's name element*, not the town. Richard Rees may be the 1828 testator or a relative.

### 2b. `Rice Laugharne` → 14 records; the spelling variant adds one genuine testator

- **PROB 11/2185/47 — Will of *Herbert Henry Rice of Laugharne*, Carmarthen, proved 7 Jan 1854** (vol. 2, quire 51–100). The one genuine surname-Rice (variant) of the town.
- **C 2/Eliz/O2/2 — *Owen v Ap Rice*, Eliz I.** Plaintiff Hugh Owen; defendant **John ap Rice**, to recover title deeds of *a messuage and 200 acres of land in Laugharne*, the plaintiff's inheritance. Genuine land in the town, but "Rice" here is a **patronymic** (ap Rice), and the object names **no house**.

### 2c. `Rhys Laugharne` → 1 record; a collision

- **STAC 8/22/1 — *Attorney General v Adams*, May 1614.** Concerns *ejecting* **Rhys Phillippes of Laugharne**. "Rhys" is a **forename**; the surname is Phillippes. No genuine surname-Rhys.

## 3. The finding

**The occupancy-spine "Rees → Island House" association is UNSOURCED from TNA Discovery.** That is the same honest outcome as Prydderch (Doc 193) and White (Doc 195), and reporting it is the point of the pass, not a failure.

Genuine **Rees/Rice figures of the town** do exist across nearly four centuries — from a patronymic land-holder under Elizabeth to four Georgian-and-Victorian testators — but **not one object names a house**, still less Island House. They are wills and land suits about people and property *in the town and parish*, and the occupancy-spine claim that a Rees held *Island House* draws no support from any of them.

### The collisions caught (method value)

A single surname sweep across three spellings reproduced the **same three collision archetypes** the White sweep found, and added a fourth structural observation:

1. **"Rees / Rice / Rhys" as a Welsh forename** — the dominant trap, exactly parallel to Doc 175's finding that "Abra" is a forename. Rees Rothereche (E 214/1079, 1608), Rees Phillippes and Rees Rotherch (STAC 8/233/14; STAC 8/248/16–17), Rees Havard the Calvinistic-Methodist minister at the Tabernacle (HO 129/589/1/42, 1851 Religious Census), Rees ap Ho' ap Gruffith (E 210/4758, 1481), Rees Rhytherch JP (STAC 8/21/10), Rhys Phillippes (STAC 8/22/1), Rice Retherch and Rice Phillips (the E 134 *Maunsell v Retherch* series, 1611–22), Rice Lloyd (STAC 5/P57/7), and "Rice Ruddy alias Rice Prudderow" (C 7/441/17, 1657). In every one, "Rees/Rice/Rhys" is a **given name**, not the surname sought.
2. **"Rees" as a middle name** — **Thomas Rees Thomas**, Major R.M., of Laugharne (surname *Thomas*; PROB 11/2252/153, 1857); and **Levi Rees Davies** of Laugharne (surname *Davies*; medal card BT 351/1/33893).
3. **"Laugharne" as a personal name, not the town** — **Sir William Philipps *Laugharne* Philipps**, the defendant in *Rees v Edwardes* (C 13/376/3; C 13/567/9), carries "Laugharne" as a name element; and **"John Laugharne, Pontvane"** fills two Navy Board in-letters alongside a "Lieutenant Rice" (ADM 106/676/108 and /116, 1712). This is the same Rowland/John Laugharne trap Docs 191 and 195 caught — the surname, not the place.

A **fourth, structural observation** worth logging: the "Rees/Rice" *forename* route re-surfaces, almost in its entirety, the **Prydderch / Retherch castle-and-lordship litigation already worked in Doc 193** — the *Maunsell v Retherch* depositions over "the castle or demesnes of Tallaugharne alias Laugharne," the Star Chamber *Rotherch v Phillipps Skarf* actions over "the castle and park of Laugharne," and *AG v Rhytherch*. Those items answer a "Rees" query only because "Rees/Rice" is the litigants' forename. They are **castle-and-lordship material, in the parallel branch's lane**, and are handed no further here.

Co-occurrence on an item or a page is not evidence; only the four PCC wills, the two Chancery suits and the Elizabethan `ap Rice` land plea put a genuine **Rees/Rice** in the same clause as the **town** of Laugharne, and none of them puts one in a **house**.

## 4. Branch-boundary note

These are township probate and property records (Stream E), and a substantial part of the forename-collision set concerns the **castle and lordship** (the Retherch/Maunsell/Phillipps material) — squarely the parallel branch's / castle's lane. This pass is nonetheless **in-lane for Island House**, because its whole purpose was to **back-check an Island House occupancy-spine claim**, and its result is precisely that these objects do *not* support it. The castle-lordship Retherch material and the wider Rees/Rice genealogy are handed no further; they belong to the parallel branch or an archive day if they ever become relevant on their own account.
