### Why this target

Doc 201 declared the occupancy-spine surname series "exhausted" and listed it as Skyrme–Hughes (191), Prydderch (193), White (195), Walters (197), Rees (199), Thomas (201). But the standing scope names **nine** spine surnames — *White, Prydderch, Thomas, Hughes, Skyrme, Abra, Rees, Powell, Walters* — and that list of six accounts for only eight of them (Hughes folded into Skyrme at 191; Abra settled as a forename at 175/181). **Powell was never run as a TNA-Discovery occupancy-spine sweep.** It had been touched only as one specific man — Sir Thomas Powell, 1st Bt., of Broadway (Doc 172, where the Powell→Island House association A166-2 was *dissolved*) and again at Doc 183 (Broadway *bought* from a Powell descendant). Those docs settled the Broadway MP; they did not test whether some *other*, town-based Powell line sits behind the Island House occupancy tradition. This run closes that one open member of the series, using the proven-readable catalogue route, and stays strictly in the Island House lane.

### Five-line finding

1. **Every "Powell Laugharne" hit read this pass is a collision.** The genuine Powells that surface are *of the county, not the town*: Morgan Phillip Powell, Gentleman **of Llanbadarn Odwyn, Cardiganshire** (PCC will 1621, **PROB 11/138/243**); William Powell **of Camberwell, Surrey** (PCC will 1813, **PROB 11/1545/445**); and a Powell acting as **estate steward to the Harley family at Llanvihangel, Monmouthshire**, 1744–69 (**PRO 30/20/24/1**). All read at object level on the server-rendered catalogue.
2. **The one genuine Laugharne record surfaced by the sweep contains no Powell at all.** *CARMARTHENSHIRE: Laugharne Castle, Cyffic Park — Proceedings (Toy v. Mansell) and inquisitions as to the decay of the castle, 13 & 21 James I* (**E 178/5069**, 1615–1624) matched only by page-level co-occurrence. Its parties are **Toy v. Mansell**; its subject is the Castle. It belongs to the Castle/lordship lane (Docs 192/200), **not** Island House.
3. **BOUNDED NEGATIVE — the 'Powell → Island House' occupancy-spine link is UNSOURCED from the open TNA surface.** Not one Powell read this pass ties even to the *town* of Laugharne, let alone to the house. This is the same honest outcome reached for White (195), Prydderch (193), Walters (197), Rees (199) and Thomas (201).
4. **The only real Powell family of the Laugharne district is the Powells of Broadway** (Sir Thomas Powell, 1st Bt., and descendants), already documented at Docs 172/183 — seated at **Broadway**, a separate house about a mile and a half from the town, *not* Island House, and with the Island House association already dissolved. This pass confirms no *second*, town-based Powell line exists to revive that claim.
5. **COMPLETION — Powell was the last un-swept spine surname.** With it tested, the desk-runnable TNA-Discovery occupancy-spine surname series is now **literally** complete: Skyrme–Hughes (191), Prydderch (193), White (195), Walters (197), Rees (199), Thomas (201), Powell (203). Every one produced genuine surname-holders somewhere in the record and, in every case, a bounded negative to the house.

### Method note (for repeat)

The 'Powell Laugharne' surface reproduces the co-occurrence trap in **two flavours at once**: county-Powells whose *residence* collides on the name (Cardiganshire, Surrey, Monmouthshire), and genuine Laugharne records that carry *no Powell* and match only at page/record level (Toy v. Mansell). Reading each object's stated residence, and holding the department filter (`PROB 11` for wills where residence is declared, `C`/`E` for suits and commissions where the subject line names the place), is what separates them.

**Unattended-run limitation, recorded honestly:** the Discovery *results-page* count route the earlier sweeps used (the JS browser, which returns "N hits" figures) was not available on this unattended pass, and `web_fetch` is provenance-gated, so **no hit-counts were taken**. Objects were surfaced by search and read individually via the server-rendered `beta.nationalarchives.gov.uk/catalogue/id/…` pages, which render cleanly without a JS browser. The finding — collisions only, no town/house Powell — is robust on the objects read; a future attended pass with the JS browser could add the exact PROB/C/E counts to firm the negative from "none found" to "N examined, none tie".
