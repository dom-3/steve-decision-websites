## 1. The wheat field photo (optional — page is fine without it)

**I can't source this one.** The sandbox blocks outbound downloads, and I have no
image generation here. So it needs two minutes from you, or it doesn't happen — and
the page is complete either way, so there's no rush.

If you want it, the free-license route is genuinely free for commercial use:

- **unsplash.com** or **pexels.com** → search *"wheat field"*, *"barley field"*,
  *"golden wheat"*, *"harvest field"*
- Both licences permit commercial use with no attribution required. Download the
  large size.

Save it as exactly:

```
~/Documents/steve-site/images/bakery/wheat-field.jpg
```

Then resize it — they come huge:

```bash
cd ~/Documents/steve-site/images/bakery && sips -Z 1400 wheat-field.jpg
```

It appears automatically. No file, and the block renders as a clean text panel
instead — deliberate-looking, not a gap.

**Why this one's fine when the borrowed photos weren't.** A wheat field is
atmosphere. It isn't a picture of our bakehouse, our hands or our bread, so it makes
no claim about our process or premises — and on a free licence there's no copyright
exposure. That was the specific objection before, and it doesn't apply here. Keep the
alt text generic though: don't caption it as our field or our mill's field.

## 2. The flour spec sheet — still a live compliance item

Worth an email regardless of the photos. Karen's ruling
(`globe-brand-tokens.md`, 22 Jul) has two items waiting on the mill's answer:

- **"stoneground"** — approved wording is *"Flour: stoneground, from [mill name]"*.
  The mill is named on the page now; the word is held back until they confirm it for
  the product we actually buy. Cotswold mill both stoneground and roller-milled.
- **"No improvers, no emulsifiers"** — on hold. Commercial flour often arrives
  containing ascorbic acid (E300), which *is* an improver, so the claim can be false
  through no fault of the baker. Item 4 releases the moment the baker and mill answer.

> **Subject:** Flour spec — The Globe Bakery, Laugharne
>
> Hi [name],
>
> Thanks again for the call. One quick thing: could you confirm which flour we're
> buying, whether it's **stoneground**, and whether it contains any improver such as
> ascorbic acid (E300)?
>
> We're describing it on our new bakery page and we'd rather say nothing than say
> something slightly wrong. A spec sheet would be ideal.
>
> Thanks,
> Dominic Pullen — The Globe, Duncan Street, Laugharne, SA33 4SA · 01994 427546

File the reply with the compliance register — it's what lets those two lines go on
the page.
