### 2a. STAC 5/O5/17 — *Ap Owen v Prytherch* (1590 Nov 17 – 1591 Nov 16)
- Cause code SCEL-09603. Documents: bill, answers.
- **Plaintiff:** Henry ap Owen of Upton, gent.
- **Defendants:** "**Rus Prethigh [Rees Prytherch] of Laugharne**, John Butler and others."
- **County:** Pembrokeshire, Wales.
- **Subject:** riots, assaults and lying in wait for Henry ap Owen on his way to a wood where he had a sparrowhawk breeding; assault on his servant guarding the hawk.
- Regnal year 33 Eliz I. Note cites **Ifan ab Owen Edwards, *A Catalogue of Star Chamber Proceedings relating to Wales* (Board of Celtic Studies, 1929), p.133.** Not digitised.

This is a **genuine early attestation of a Prydderch/Prytherch resident at Laugharne** (c.1590) — but the case sits in the Pembrokeshire orbit (an Upton plaintiff, a hawk-nest dispute) and says nothing of Island House.

### 2b. STAC 5/P57/7 — *Prytherch v Lloyd* (1601 Nov 17 – 1602 Nov 16)
- Cause code SCEL-16847. Documents: bill only.
- **Plaintiff:** "**James Prytherch of Hawkesbrook, JP, late deputy-steward of the Crown Lordship of Llanstephan**."
- **Defendants:** Rice Lloyd gent, David Boult, David Barrett, Jane Lloyd and others.
- **County:** Carmarthenshire, Wales.
- **Subject:** assault on James Prytherch by Rice Lloyd, David Barrett and others "**at St Rects in the Lordship of Laugharne**" over ill will arising from fines and amercements at a Court Baron; terrifying county officers off arresting Lloyd and Barrett after a Carmarthen Sessions conviction; rescuing men pressed by the deputy-lieutenant for service in Ireland.
- Regnal year 44 Eliz I. Note cites **Edwards (1929), p.49.** Not digitised.

## 3. The finding

**The occupancy-spine "Prydderch → Island House" association is UNSOURCED from TNA Discovery.** Reporting that honestly is the point of the pass, not a failure.

Neither of the only two Prytherch×Laugharne records ties the surname to the house:

1. **Rees Prytherch (fl. 1590)** is a Laugharne *resident*, but the only fact about him is his part in a Pembrokeshire hawk-nest riot. No house, no office at Laugharne, no Island House.
2. **James Prytherch (fl. 1601–02)** is a **near-collision**: he is "of Hawkesbrook" and deputy-steward of the Crown Lordship of **Llanstephan** — a *different* lordship. His only connection to Laugharne is that the assault on him *happened* "at St Rects in the Lordship of Laugharne." A surname sharing a page — or here a place-of-incident — with "Laugharne" is not evidence of a Laugharne man, and certainly not of an Island House man. This is the same trap Doc 191 caught with Rowland Laugharne (a surname, not the town), logged here so it cannot enter the record as an Island House Prydderch.

So the two earliest documentary Prytherches in the Laugharne field are a town resident with no house tie and a Llanstephan officer with no Laugharne residence at all. Whatever put "Prydderch" on the Island House occupancy spine, it was **not** these records.
