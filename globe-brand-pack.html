Typography — one type family

Clear, legible, quiet. Never set long copy in hairline weights or all-caps.

The gold thread

One warm gold runs through everything — used sparingly as the accent (wordmark, rules, dividers), expressed in each branch's own light (deep on the dark restaurant, wheaten on the bakery). It's the visual signature that ties the family together.

Provenance voice & real photography

We speak with named provenance and few, well-chosen words. We show real photographs — one hero, never a collage, natural light, the product and the place doing the talking. Real text, never image-only menus.

05 · How we speak voice & tone

Understated, provenance-led, warm.

Say less, name more. Let the supplier, the place and the dish carry it. Confident without shouting; warm without waffle. The humour of the Globe is welcome; hype never is.

Do
  • Name it — suppliers, town, the maker.
  • Few words, well chosen.
  • Speak of the season and the tide.
  • Be generous and human.
  • Let the photo and the dish talk.
Don't
  • Hype, superlatives or exclamation marks.
  • Corporate or salesy phrasing.
  • Invented heritage or claims we can't prove.
  • Long, hedged sentences.
  • Branch name without "The Globe".

How it sounds in each light

Restaurant — "Whole Towy sewin, brown butter & cockles. A few weeks each summer. Booked, small, exact."

Bakery — "Bread baked in Laugharne, every morning."

House of Culture — "Walk Dylan's Laugharne. The real places, the real story — with the town as your guide."

07 · How we look layout & usage rules

Legible first, decorative second.

  • Globe-first lockup on every piece. The branch name never stands alone.
  • Gold used sparingly — accent, rules, wordmark — not fills.
  • One hero image per piece; generous space; everything on a grid.
  • Real, selectable text for menus and prices — never an image-only menu.
  • Each branch uses its own palette from §04; the master neutrals + gold tie them.
  • Images embedded, not linked (Cloudinary isn't serving publicly yet).
08 · Using this pack marketing plan + automations

The source of truth everything references.

This pack sets how we look and sound; the marketing plan sets what we say and when. Every output — post, page, menu, sign, packaging, email, ad — is built to this. Automations pull the brand tokens below (colours, fonts, lockup, voice rules) so templated content stays on-brand without a human re-checking each time.

Who builds to it

The desk sets the system. Brush designs pieces to it. Bob builds web to it. Nia posts & advertises to it. Mererid keeps the culture layer true. Nobody invents a new look — they express this one.

The automation rule

Any automated output loads the tokens + the branch's palette + the voice do/don'ts, applies the Globe-first lockup, and references the marketing plan for timing & message. On-brand by default.
