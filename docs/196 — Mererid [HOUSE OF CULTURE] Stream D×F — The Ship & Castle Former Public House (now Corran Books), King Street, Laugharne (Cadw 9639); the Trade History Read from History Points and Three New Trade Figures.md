### 2. Five-line finding

1. **Cadw 9639 pins the fabric, not the story.** Grade II, listed 25 Sep 1986, Commercial, group value only; late-C18/early-C19 origins with early-C20 reglazing; attached to the Manse (9640) near the crossroads. The listing says nothing of an inn, Dylan, or Corran Books — logged as a scope fact so the trade history is tiered on its own sources.
2. **The inn phase (History Points, T3):** the former **Ship & Castle Inn** hosted, in Victorian times, **property auctions, inquests and Oddfellows meetings** — a genuine public-house-as-civic-room function; and in the early 1890s **cookery lessons** in its club room from a Miss Perry of University College, Cardiff.
3. **A named estate ritual and a court case:** **J.A.R. Broadwood of the Broadway Estate, Llansadurnen** audited the estate's rents at the Ship & Castle each year, tenants dining afterward; in **March 1894** Broadwood and **E.W.H. Peel of Fernhill, Laugharne** were tried at St Clear's petty sessions for being drunk and disorderly at the inn — the case, tried amid *"much merriment"* over *"county gentlemen"* in the dock, was dismissed.
4. **The 1914 closure ties the Ship & Castle to Brown's:** landlord **William Griffiths** bought **Brown's Hotel** across the road in 1914, closed the Ship & Castle and moved to live at Brown's — a direct documentary link between this building and Brown's (Cadw 9638, Doc 194), and the end of the Ship & Castle's licensed trade.
5. **The modern afterlife:** through the 20th century the ground-floor front rooms held various shops, **including a Barclays Bank branch**; in **1982** the writer **George Tremlett** opened the bookshop **Corran Books** here, giving the building its present name.
