### Five-line finding

1. **Identity confirmed at primary level.** Island House Restoration Ltd is a private limited company, no. **12452655**, **incorporated 10 February 2020** (model articles, statement of capital £1,000), status **Active**, nature of business **SIC 41100 — Development of building projects**. So the vehicle is a *building-development / restoration* company, not (as at 2026) a trading hotel or restaurant — consistent with SAVE's "boutique hotel/restaurant/bar" as a *future* use, not a current activity.

2. **The owner firmed, and a hierarchy the register never had.** Two directors, both appointed 10 Feb 2020, no resignations: **Philip Bond** (b. Oct 1942, resident England) and **Stephen Clive Kirkwood** (b. March 1953, resident Wales). The persons-with-significant-control page adds what the desk did not know: **Philip Bond is the majority owner** (shares *and* votes >50% but <75%); **Kirkwood is the minority** (>25% but ≤50%). The existing entity named both partners without hierarchy; Bond, not Kirkwood, is the principal owner of the vehicle. Kirkwood is the "Steve" / "Stephen Kirkwood" named as steward on the Cadw Open Doors excavation record (Doc 185).

3. **Incorporation ≠ acquisition (scope-guard).** The register dates only the company's **formation** (10 Feb 2020). SAVE's "**acquired March 2020**" (Doc 185) is a separate, later date the register does **not** confirm. The two are coherent — form the vehicle in February, complete the purchase in March — but must not be conflated; the acquisition fact stays at its SAVE / T2 tier, **not** upgraded on the strength of the incorporation date.

4. **No charges on file (bounded negative).** There are **no registered charges or mortgages** against the company. The purchase and restoration are not evidenced by any registered charge, so Companies House gives **no independent acquisition date or financing record**. Any mortgage/financing evidence would sit in the Land Registry title, not here — an archive/paid route.

5. **The Globe House address is administrative and post-dates the purchase (anti-conflation guard).** At incorporation the registered office was **Hen Capel Mair, Saw Mills Lane, St Clears (SA33 4EN)** — an agent/accountant address; it changed to **Globe House, Market Street, Laugharne (SA33 4SA)** only on **3 May 2022**. So the Globe-address link is a *2022 administrative* registered-office change, **not** evidence of any Island House–Globe operational link and **not** the house's own address (Island House is at Wogan Street / The Grist, Docs 185/187). Do not read the address into the House-of-Culture branch's Globe material.

**Modern-fate texture:** the company twice entered compulsory strike-off (First Gazette notices 11 Jan 2022 and 6 Jun 2023), both discontinued (23 Feb 2022, 7 Jun 2023); it files small-company "total exemption full accounts" (ARD 31 March; latest to 31 March 2025) and remains Active. Administratively rocky (two rescued strike-offs, typically late filing) but continuing — consistent with a long-running restoration by a small development vehicle.
