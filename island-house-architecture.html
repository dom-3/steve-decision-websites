Island House · Website Architecture

How the website works

The blueprint for the Island House site — built around one job: tell the restoration story as it happens and grow an audience who follow it. A living journal, not a static brochure. The history is done and fact-checked.

The rule for every page: sell the restoration itself — the process is the attraction. Chapter by chapter, evidence-true, beautifully shot. Give people a reason to follow, then to visit when it opens.

4 · How it's built

Story-first site + CMS

Next/Astro on Vercel with a simple CMS so journal chapters ship without a developer.

Media (Cloudinary)

The photography, film and time-lapse served fast — heavy media handled properly.

Audience (Airtable + email)

The journal following captured and nurtured; press list managed.

The record (Supabase, later)

The inspection/documentation data + the interactive “one area” dashboard when we build it.

The site and the documentation system share one capture — a photo taken on site can feed the record, the story and the marketing at once (“capture once, use everywhere”).

5 · What we deliberately leave for later

  • The interactive “one area” restoration dashboard (build after the standards are signed off).
  • A grants / partnership microsite if the discovery route is pursued.
  • Booking / stays — added when the hospitality side nears opening.
