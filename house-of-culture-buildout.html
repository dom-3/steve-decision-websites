0 · The living loop — how it stays true

Everything here updates, and it updates safely. This is the cycle that turns new research into live experience without ever letting an unproven claim through.

1 · DigResearch runsMererid works the backlog continuously; new facts enter the chain of custody, tiered.
2 · ProveThe gateA fact's status (Verified → Refuted) decides whether the beat it feeds can go live.
3 · FindThe indexEvery doc logged the moment it's written — nothing lost, nothing paraphrased.
4 · ApproveYour tick"Approved for site" — a human gate. Nothing reaches the public without it.
5 · ShipOne publisherThe Master desk publishes; no desk overwrites another.
6 · CorrectPropagateWhen a claim changes, the retraction sweeps every place it lived. Same day.

The instant fact-check sits inside this loop. Because every claim is already graded and sourced, an AI can answer a visitor's question from the same graded library — and refuse to bluff when the record is silent. The honesty engine isn't a feature bolted on; it's the loop, made conversational.

5 · The interaction psychology

Why people engage, spend and come back — designed in, not left to chance.

Curiosity, then reward

The QR asks a question ("what happened here?") and answers it. Completing a story unlocks the dossier — depth as the reward, never a badge for its own sake.

Free earns trust

A genuine free gift lowers the guard and raises goodwill — so the later spend feels like thanks, not extraction. The provenance shop then converts trust into a purchase people feel good about.

Place the spend early

"We'll save you a loaf" plants a purchase hours ahead — a small commitment that completes itself on the walk home. Service that happens to sell.

Reverence where it's due

The game goes silent at the grave. Knowing when not to gamify is what earns the respect that makes the playful parts acceptable.
