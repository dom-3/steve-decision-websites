## Findings

**1. The occupancy is firmed and dated: Frederick Wienholt Esq., J.P., at Island House c.1863–1881.**
The earliest firm attestation is *The Welshman*, 16 October 1863, p.5 (read in full): at a meeting of the established Church in the **Town Hall, Laugharne**, "Morgan Jones, Esq., of Llanmilo … proposed, and **Frederick Wienholt, Esq., Island House, in the township**, seconded" a resolution against a church-rate substitute. He was already one of the parish's principal inhabitants. (The same meeting names William Norton Esq. of *Laugharne Castle* and the Vicar, Rev. J. N. Harrison.)

The end anchor is *The Cambrian*, 29 April 1881, p.8, Family Notices (read in full): **"At Island House, Laugharne, Frederick Wienholt Esq., J.P., Laugharne, Carmarthenshire, and Greenfield, Lanarkshire, eldest son of the late John Birkett Wienholt, Esq."** This is his death notice. It newly establishes his **J.P.** rank and a **second seat at Greenfield, Lanarkshire**, and names his father.

**2. The identity join — Doc 177's "Wrinbolt" resolves to WIENHOLT.**
Doc 177 (the Skyrme–Hughes marriage) read a badly OCR'd 1858 notice: "Frederick **Wrinbolt**, Esq., of London, eldest son of the late **J. Birkett Wrinbolt**, Esq., the Castle, Laugharne, to **Abra Hughes**, only daughter and heiress of the late William Skyrme, Esq., of Laugharne." The 1881 death notice gives the identical father-styling — "eldest son of the late **John Birkett Wienholt**, Esq." The forename, the father's forenames, the Laugharne locus and the "eldest son of the late …" formula all match. **The 1858 groom is the Frederick Wienholt of Island House.** Tiered **Verified** (not Verified-strong: the 1858 surname is OCR-garbled and that notice was not re-read direct this pass).

**3. Abra Hughes became Mrs Wienholt, mistress of Island House.**
It follows that the 1858 bride, Abra Hughes, became mistress and — after Frederick's April 1881 death — widow-occupant of Island House. "Mrs. Wienholt, Island House" is independently attested as a patroness of the Laugharne National & Infant Schools building bazaar in **1867** (*Tenby Observer* 12 Sep 1867; also *The Welshman* 6 Sep 1867), and, as widow, as the **sole funder of the chancel work at the 1882 restoration of Llandawke church** (*Western Mail* 2 Sep 1882, dup. *Weekly Mail* 9 Sep 1882) — which ties the Wienholts of Island House to the Llandawke fabric worked at Doc 164a.

This materially advances, but does **not** close, the long-standing puzzle F177-3 / thread `recbuyBlxyy2kWoHA`: *how* a woman surnamed Hughes is the "only daughter and heiress" of a Skyrme. That remains **Open**. Per the standing prohibition (Doc 179), she is **not** written as "Abra Skyrme" or "née Skyrme."

**4. A named Island House household member.**
The Wienholt household included a resident housekeeper, **Elizabeth Ebsworth**, named in a February 1879 Carmarthenshire libel case as "the housekeeper for Mr Frederick We[i]nholt, Island House, Laugharne" (*Cardiff Times* 15 Feb 1879, dup. *South Wales Daily News* 12 Feb 1879 — snippet level).

**5. The succession bracketed.**
The 1892 Laugharne vicar's-funeral wreath list names, in one item, both "the Misses Wienholt" (daughters) and **"Mr Hughes-Garbett, Island House"** (*Carmarthen Journal* 8 Apr 1892, p.6 — snippet level). So the resident of Island House had passed from the Wienholts to Percy Hughes-Garbett by 1892, consistent with Docs 207/214 (Hughes-Garbett resident c.1890–1893). No kinship between the two families is asserted.

**Resulting occupancy spine (this limb):** Frederick Wienholt (m. Abra Hughes 1858; at Island House by 1863; J.P.; d. April 1881) → his widow "Mrs Wienholt" and "the Misses Wienholt" into the early 1890s → Percy Hughes-Garbett resident by 1890/1892.
