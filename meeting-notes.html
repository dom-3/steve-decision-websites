🥖 Bakery team meeting

Steve · Dominic · Fred (baker) & bakery team · Alice (admin/ordering) · Carl

The headline

  • We're a baker — so we start with bread. Bread is the spine; pastries are the profit add-on but not what people come for. Keep it simple — “sprint before you can walk”.
  • This week = trial bakes + photography. Open for real next week (from Tuesday) — bank-holiday weekend.

The range we launch with — bread first

Traditional white — hand-shaped, in baskets
8-grain / multiseed
Organic stone-ground (the organic angle)
Traditional wholemeal
Cotswold Crunch (dark, crusty)
Sourdough — large & small (tin / flower-pot)
Soft rolls — every variation
French sticks / baguettes
  • Sizes: 800g (family) and 400g (individual); square tins, baskets and flower-pots.
  • Cheese & marmite as an occasional variation (the only additions).

Pastries — the add-on, coming in

  • Croissants: naked, pistachio, chocolate (3 variations) — still being perfected (freezer/proving tests).
  • Danish: apple, apricot, black cherry.
  • ~12 types → ~24 variations over time. Coffee + croissants served in the wine bar area to ~12:00.

How we sell

  • Collection only — no delivery to start.
  • Online pre-order + phone orders (use the existing number) so we bake to demand, not blind — this is where the website earns its keep and cuts waste.
  • Subscription/pre-order kept simple — no loyalty cards or complex incentives yet.
  • Sell-out model, twice-a-day idea (French style): open 8:30, again around 12:00.

Opening & hours

  • Trading days: closed Sunday & Monday; build up Tuesday→Saturday (start Tue & Sat).
  • Bake 5–6am, finished ~10am; shop opens 8:30.
  • Day-one target: ~60 big loaves (12 of each of ~5 types) + ~half that in small loaves + 2 trays of rolls on show.

Brand & naming

  • Company: Globe Unlimited → trades as The Globe (wine bar + bakery).
  • Official name on documents: Globe Bakery; marketed as artisan bakery.
  • Social stays with the wine bar for now (~1,000 followers) — no separate bakery page yet. Laura to run Facebook; Dominic to guide + automate.

The story / ethos (the marketing gold)

  • Organic stone-ground, Cotswold flour, no additives — no sugar, no softeners, no numbers. Health-led, “close to what God made”.
  • Gather testimonials — esp. gluten-sensitive customers who can eat it. Powerful proof.

Photography plan

  • Dominic shoots each bake individually + the bulk (to show we can do volume), photographic quality, on our own cameras (avoid paying Nicky if we can).
  • Shoot in the bakery (overhead lights) + the courtyard (plate on slate). Photo of the bakers in whites, with their bread — home feel, “it's our bakery”.
  • Automate it: baker snaps a photo → uploads to a session → Dominic edits → auto-posts. Minimal effort for the team. CapCut/TikTok exports without the logo.

Packaging

  • Micro-perforation poly bags for bread; large clear zip bags for croissants/freezing; ties/seals.
  • Paper band printed “Globe Bakery” (same on all) + a per-bread sticker label to differentiate — an upper-class feel.
