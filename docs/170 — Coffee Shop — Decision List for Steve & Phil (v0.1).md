## D1 — Is the coffee shop a *door* or a *business*?
**Recommend:** a door. One P&L with the bakery, one kitchen, one EPOS, one brand.
**Trade-off:** you lose the ability to judge the coffee shop on its own numbers — and you will never be able to close it cleanly if it underperforms, because by then it is load-bearing for the bakery.

## D2 — Does coffee GP carry the bread, or does bread justify the coffee?
**Recommend:** treat coffee GP as the engine and bread as the reason to come — model them as one daytime line, never separately.
**Trade-off:** if that thesis is wrong the whole daytime case collapses at once rather than failing in one half, so CS-03 has to stress-test it against February, not August.

## D3 — Opening hours, and how hard they bite the evening
**Recommend:** open on the bakery's existing production clock rather than a café clock — early, closing mid-afternoon.
**Trade-off:** you catch the morning trail walkers and the collection trade but concede the 4–6pm tea slot to the Boathouse tearoom.

## D4 — Roaster: the Welsh route, or the best cup available?
**Recommend:** hold the west Wales / Welsh sourcing route already agreed in principle, subject to CS-04 proving delivery frequency and consistency.
**Trade-off:** a Welsh roaster is worth real money in story and in the House of Culture framing; a national roaster is cheaper per kilo and more reliable to Laugharne in winter.

## D5 — Espresso machine: capex, or roaster loan?
**Recommend:** loan-with-contract for year one, purchase at review.
**Trade-off:** the loan protects cash and ties you to one roaster's beans; buying costs c.£8–15k up front (CS-06 to firm) and keeps you free.

## D6 — Takeaway for walkers: how far do we go?
**Recommend:** hot drinks and whole bakery items to go; **no** pre-packed filled food at launch.
**Trade-off:** filled rolls are the obvious trail sale and the obvious margin — but pre-packing them for direct sale drops you straight into PPDS / Natasha's Law labelling, and that is a process burden on a small kitchen, not a sticker. Karen (CS-10) prices this before it is reopened.

## D7 — Where it physically sits, and the walker's route through the building
**Recommend:** service off the existing counter, with a decision needed on whether walkers can be served without crossing the evening room.
**Trade-off:** a separate hatch protects the evening set-up and costs build money; one counter is cheaper and means muddy boots in the dining room at 2pm.

## D8 — Brand: how visible is "coffee shop" as a name?
**Recommend:** no separate name. It is *The Globe* in the daytime — the third face of the Globe Studio system after Wine Bar dark and Bakery light.
**Trade-off:** a named café would be easier to market and find on Google; an unnamed door keeps every daytime customer inside the Globe's brand and makes the evening upsell natural rather than a hand-off between two businesses.

## D9 — The House of Culture conversion mechanic
**Recommend:** the coffee stop is the day-loop's morning anchor and mid-walk rest, and it carries **one** explicit route into an evening booking.
**Trade-off:** whatever that mechanic is (a card with the coffee, a walk-in book at the counter, a discount) it costs either margin or staff attention at the busiest moment of the daytime shift. Blueprint v0.4 already stages *arrive → coffee → reading room → trailhead* — we build on that spine rather than re-cutting it.

## D10 — Launch timing against the bakery
**Recommend:** coffee follows the bakery going live, not alongside it.
**Trade-off:** sequencing protects one kitchen from two simultaneous launches, but means the bakery runs its first months without the high-GP line that makes it work.
