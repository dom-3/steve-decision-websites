Globe Bakery · Website Architecture

How the website works

The blueprint for the bakery site — kept deliberately simple, built around one job: sell bread for collection. This is what we build from with each persona once the iMac's set up. Scaled off the sites we liked (Alex Gooch is the bar).

The rule for every page: lead with the bread, show the price, keep it simple, let people order for collection in two clicks. Contained hero (no image bleeding past the screen), photography-led, organic + Welsh. Pastries and everything else are secondary.

1 · The sitemap

Lean on purpose — six pages, everything one tap from home. Nothing that doesn't help sell bread.

HomeBread front-and-centre · one clear “Order for collection” button
The Bread ▸ OrderThe range + prices, order for collection
Weekly BoxSimple subscription (loaf + pastry)
PastriesCroissants & Danish — the add-on

2 · Page by page

Each page top-to-bottom — the blocks in order. The lead persona for each is on the right.

1
HomeMarcus + Delphine + Cass
Get them to the bread and to “order for collection” fast.
  • Header: Globe Bakery logo · nav (Bread · Weekly Box · Pastries · Story · Visit) · a bold “Order for collection” button.
  • Hero (contained): one beautiful loaf, headline “Real bread, baked in Laugharne”, subline “Organic, stone-ground, no additives”, primary CTA Order bread. No full-bleed overflow.
  • This week's bread: a tidy grid — photo + name + price + “Add to order”. The heart of the page.
  • Why our bread: three short proofs — organic stone-ground · Cotswold flour · no additives.
  • How it works: order online → we bake it → collect Tue–Sat.
  • The bakers: the photo of the pair with their bread → “Our story”.
  • Footer: hours, phone, the Globe wine bar link, socials.
2
The Bread — OrderSol + Marcus
The engine — browse, order, pick a collection slot, done.
  • The full range: each loaf — photo, name, size (400g / 800g), price, “Add to order”.
  • Order summary: what's in the basket, running total.
  • Collection slot: pick the day + time (Tue–Sat, 8:30 or ~12) with the order cut-off shown.
  • Checkout: pay or reserve — simple, no account needed. Collection only, no delivery.
  • Why pre-order: a gentle line — “order ahead so we bake it fresh for you” (this is what stops us baking blind).
3
Weekly BoxSol
Turn a one-off into a habit — kept dead simple.
  • One clear offer: a weekly loaf + pastry box, collected each week.
  • Simple controls: pause / skip / cancel any time. No tiers or loyalty gimmicks (per Steve).
  • One CTA: “Start your weekly box”.
4
PastriesDelphine + Cass
The profitable add-on — present it, don't let it upstage the bread.
  • Croissants — naked, pistachio, chocolate; Danish — apple, apricot, black cherry.
  • “Add to your order” — folds into the same collection basket.
  • Note: coffee & croissants in the wine bar to ~12:00 (a soft cross-link).
5
Our StoryDelphine
The reason to trust and love it — the premium, human, Welsh story.
  • The ethos: organic, stone-ground, Cotswold flour, no additives — no sugar, no softeners.
  • The bakers, in their whites — the “it's our bakery”, home-baked feel.
  • Testimonials (esp. gluten-sensitive customers who can eat it).
  • Part of the Globe — a nod to the wine bar.
6
VisitMarcus + Nia
Everything to actually come and collect.
  • Hours: Tue–Saturday, from 8:30 (and the ~12 second bake). Closed Sun & Mon.
  • Location + map; parking; the phone number for phone orders.
  • How collection works — order ahead, or walk-in while it lasts.

3 · The order journey (the money path)

Two clicks from “nice loaf” to “it's reserved”. This is the whole point of the site.

Step 1
Browse the breadHome or The Bread — photos + prices.
Step 2
Add + pick a slotChoose loaves + a collection day/time.
Step 3
Pay or reserveQuick checkout, no account needed.
Step 4
Collect freshWe baked it because you ordered it.

Repeat customers convert to the Weekly Box — same path, set once.

4 · How it's built (simple on purpose)

Shopify — the engine

Handles the shop, payments, tax and local pickup / collection out of the box, plus a subscription app for the weekly box. We don't hand-build a store.

A clean, bread-first theme

Premium, photography-led, fast — Alex Gooch level. Contained hero, price always visible.

Photography (Cloudinary)

The bread shots served crisp and optimised. Bakers snap → Dominic edits → auto-posts.

Orders → the bakery

Make.com pushes each order to the team (so they bake the right amount); Klaviyo for the weekly-box emails.

Launch on Shopify to be live fast; a custom front-end (Astro on Vercel, headless) can come later if we ever want it. No delivery, no accounts, no clutter — simple.

5 · What we deliberately leave for later

  • Expand the pastry range; a proper coffee / wine-bar tie-in page.
  • Trade & wholesale — a simple “trade enquiry” once we're steady. (Seed it early with partners like Lynette, whose letting-agent / Airbnb contacts could take regular bread + croissant drops as a buy-in.)
  • A separate bakery social presence (stays with the wine bar for now).
  • Delivery — only if demand clearly calls for it.
