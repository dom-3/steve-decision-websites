### The five-line finding

1. **Cadw 9650 re-read at object level (independent of the pre-index read).** The FullReport endpoint confirms: **Grade II\*, designated 30 November 1966**; an *early-C18 Georgian double-pile* town house; 2-storey with basement and attic; 5-2-window pebbledash front; steep slate roofs; central pediment; an *exceptional central late-Baroque doorcase* with broad bracket cornice, foliage entablature and **Corinthian reeded pilasters**; a *fine early-C18 dog-leg staircase* with fluted banisters and foliage ornament to the tread-ends; interior **cockle-shell niches**; twin semicircular arches dividing front and rear piles on the first-floor landing; original 7-bay pegged-timber roof. The W range (formerly the servants' hall) was "in poor condition" and the house "undergoing restoration" at the **January 1986** inspection. **The History and Reason-for-designation sections are BLANK** — the statutory record is fabric-description only, and does **not** name the street.

2. **Coflein/RCAHMW NPRN 17401 read direct — and it is NOT a Cadw copy.** Unlike the bare Island House Coflein stubs of Docs 187/189 ("No description available"), the RCAHMW record carries its **own description in different words**: *"18th century early. Rough cast. 2 storey. Double saddle slate roof. Central gable. Doorcase with pediment and pilasters. Fine stair. Double archway on 1st floor landing is noteworthy."* Coflein "rough cast / double saddle slate roof / central gable / double archway" against Cadw "pebbledash / steep slate roofs / central pediment / twin semicircular arches" is the same fabric described **independently**. The core fabric therefore stands on **two authorities** → **Verified-strong** at fact level (F202-2).

3. **The King Street address is now Verified from a national record.** Coflein titles the record **"Great House, King Street, Laugharne"**; the Cadw 9650 listing gives only "at the end of a continuous row of frontages, stepped up from the Vicarage" and never names the street. The address had rested on a Wikipedia signpost (not a source); it is now Verified from RCAHMW, independent of Cadw (F202-3). Grid **SN3015011060** (Coflein) = Cadw **SN30150 11061** — the same building, to a metre.

4. **A media asset located.** Coflein NPRN 17401's Related Archives hold a substantial RCAHMW **NMR Site File** (archive nos. **750853–750862** and beyond, across ≥5 pages) — main elevation, the doorway, a rear view, multiple interiors and the staircase; **two carry online images** (6303270 the doorway; 6249736 the elevation). An existing national visual record of the finest Georgian town house in Laugharne — an archive-day / media target for the trail (F202-5).

5. **The Benjamin West museum-provenance, recorded in a second Cadw object.** Cadw 9650 states Great House *"formerly housed a museum which included the Benjamin West painting now in the Church."* This is a **second Cadw object** (distinct from the church listing 9623, Docs 161/163a) recording the painting's local provenance and its physical route — **Great House museum → St Martin's**. Being Cadw, it is **not** the independent, non-Cadw corroboration Doc 163a still needs for the *attribution*; that stays Referenced-pending (F202-6, and see guard below).
