The Globe & The Globe Bakery — one place, one brand

The idea in a line: the bar and the bakery are the same place, the same story, the same care — so they should look and feel like one brand, not two. This page explains why, what we found on the current website, and what we'd do together to bring it up. Have a read and tell us if you're happy — you can leave notes right on the page.

The idea — it all comes off the same thing

The Globe is Laugharne's hidden gem: a bar, a kitchen and a secret garden in a building full of history on Duncan Street. The bakery isn't a separate business bolted on — it's the daytime half of the same place. Same door, same people, same care, turned to flour in the morning and wine in the evening.

Everything runs off one promise — the kitchen already says it best: “care — for ingredients, for people, and for the land that grows what we serve.” That's the Globe by night and the bakery by day. If we build both on that one idea, they reinforce each other: a customer who buys their weekly bread becomes an evening regular, and vice versa.

So the goal isn't two websites competing — it's one Globe family, consistent in look, voice and story, with two front doors.

What's already great about the Globe

We build on these — they're real and they're yours.

A genuinely rare story. The building has been a hotel, a coach station, a butcher's & abattoir (the Gleed family until 1991) and more — and it's been on screen in the BBC's Dylan in New York and Keeping Faith. Few venues have anything like it.
A memorable line. “The Hidden Gem of Laugharne · escape to our secret garden, no passport required.”
The right values, already written. The care-for-ingredients-people-and-land ethos is exactly what a premium organic bakery stands for too.
The basics work. Online table booking is easy, and the events (live music, DJs, the choir) are kept current.

What we found — the issues on the current site

Said plainly and constructively — the site is young (opened Oct 2024) and under-selling a strong venue. Fixable, mostly quickly.

The menu is pictures, not words.
Menus are uploaded as screenshots, so there are no readable dishes or prices, no food photos, and Google can't see any of it. This is the single biggest miss — and the easiest big win.
Opening hours appear nowhere.
The most-searched fact about any bar or restaurant isn't on the site — visitors have to call or guess.
The brand is pulling apart.
“Wine Bar” vs “Bar & Restaurant”, and the Instagram handle shows up two different ways across pages. Small things, but they read as not-quite-looked-after.
The great story is buried.
The heritage and the current owners' story barely feature — the thing that makes the Globe special is hardest to find.
No brand pack.
There's a lovely feel, but no written colours, fonts or logo spec to keep the bar, bakery, menus and social all consistent.

What we'd do — together

In order, quickest wins first. Nothing here is a big rebuild — it's surfacing what's already true.

1
Quick fixes (days)
  • Put opening hours on the homepage, contact page and footer.
  • Turn the menu into real text — dishes, short mouth-watering descriptions, prices — so it reads on a phone and shows up on Google.
  • Unify the name and the Instagram handle everywhere.
  • Add page titles + descriptions so search can find you.
2
The brand pack & story (1–2 weeks)
  • Write up the Globe brand pack — colours, fonts, logo, voice — so everything stays consistent (this also governs the bakery).
  • Rewrite About around the real story: the building's history, the garden, and you.
  • Add real photography — the food, the interiors, the garden.
3
One family, then launch the bakery (ongoing)
  • Bring the bakery online as part of the Globe — “by day, the bakery; by night, the bar.”
  • Cross-promote both audiences and run one simple content + events rhythm on social.
  • Local SEO so people nearby actually find you.
