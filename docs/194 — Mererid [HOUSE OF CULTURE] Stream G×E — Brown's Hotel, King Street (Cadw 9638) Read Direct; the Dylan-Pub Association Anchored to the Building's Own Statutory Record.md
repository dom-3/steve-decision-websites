### 2. What the record establishes (Verified)

**2.1 Listing and grade (F194-1).** Brown's Hotel is listed **Grade II** by Cadw (record 9638), first listed 30 November 1966, Building Class Commercial.

**2.2 Listed for group value (F194-2).** The Reasons for Listing are given simply as **"Group value."** Brown's is protected for its contribution to the King Street streetscape near the crossroads, not for individual architectural distinction — and it carries the same Grade II as its listed neighbours in that row: the Manse (9640, opposite), the Pelican (9641, opposite — worked at Doc 147), Corran Books / former Ship & Castle P.H. (9639), Elm House (9637), The Pines (9636) and the New Three Mariners P.H. (9670). This is the honest tier: the building's heritage claim is **contextual**, and the doc says so rather than inflating it.

**2.3 The fabric date and the two inscriptions (F194-3, F194-7).** The listing dates the fabric to **1752**, with a **1938 restoration** and modern alterations. Two primary inscriptions carry those facts on the building itself: a lettered panel between the upper floors bears a **shield dated 1752 and 1938**, and a plaque to the left reads, verbatim, **"This Pine End Belongs to G James 1752."** That plaque names a datable townsperson — **G James, fl. 1752** — as owner of the gable, and is registered as a lead for the King Street property/occupancy spine.

**2.4 The architecture (F194-4).** Georgian, two-storey-and-attic, three-window roughcast front; tall slate roof with lowered eaves, rendered chimney stacks and modern swept dormers; recessed 12-pane sash windows to the first floor; splayed ground-floor bays with advanced moulded cornices and sash glazing; a central porch with **Doric columns** and a boarded door.

**2.5 The Dylan-pub association, anchored to the building's own record (F194-5).** The statutory listing states, in its own text, that **"Dylan Thomas frequented the bar during his years in Laugharne and the interior is now decorated with memorabilia."** This is the point of reading the object: the town's Dylan-pub association is fixed to a **primary designation record**, and that record names **Brown's** — not the Globe. It is the correct, on-the-record home of the association. What the object supports is exactly the verb it uses — *frequented*; the wider "favourite pub / wrote here" embellishments are not adopted from this source.

### 3. What is held below Verified — honest tiering

**3.1 The bar and the chapel pulpit (F194-6, Tradition).** The listing adds that the bar **"is said to have been made out of the pulpit of the destroyed Congregational Chapel."** The statutory text flags this itself as tradition — *is said to* — so it is logged at **Tradition**, not adopted as fact. The destroyed Congregational Chapel is registered as its own entity but was not otherwise identified (date, site) this pass; that is an open lead.

**3.2 "Est. 1752" is the fabric date, not a trading-since claim (F194-8, Referenced-pending / correction).** The house trades today under the line **"est. 1752."** Read against the object, 1752 is the date of the **building's construction** (the shield and the G-James plaque both fix it), not evidence of continuous inn or hotel trading from that year. The statutory listing dates the fabric to 1752 but records **no** licensed or hotel use from that date. Secondary accounts place the change to inn/hotel use later — in the nineteenth century — but that was **not read at object level** this pass, so the "became a hotel in the C19" claim is left **Referenced-pending**, to be settled from a directory or the Corporation/licensing record on the archive day. The safe wording for the site is therefore: *the building is dated 1752; its use as an inn came later.* Anchor to the fabric date, not to a trading-since claim.

### 4. Standing-guardrail check (Doc 140)

This is a Brown's document, and Brown's **is** the correct Dylan pub — so the object was read and reported straight, with no pivot. Specifically:

- **The banned shape was avoided.** Doc 140 bans the structure "Dylan's pub was Brown's, BUT here's our Dylan link." This doc concedes and stops at Brown's; it does **not** manufacture a Globe/Dylan bridge off the back of it. The Globe is not mentioned as a Dylan site anywhere here.
- **No over-claim on the Dylan association.** Only what the listing says — that he *frequented the bar* — is tiered Verified. "Favourite pub" and any "wrote here" reading are left out.
- **The tradition is contained, not laundered.** The chapel-pulpit story is kept at Tradition because the source itself hedges it; it is not promoted to "local history."
- **No trading-since inflation.** "Est. 1752" is explicitly separated from the fabric date.
- Nothing here touches the banned Globe formulations (Butcher Beynon, "town's last butcher", "13th-century cellars", the charter numerals, the corporation wording), the siege length, or Island House.
