4 · The layer model

The same stop serves a twenty-minute walker and a three-hour obsessive. Only L1 is mandatory; everything below is a choice.

L0Orient10 seconds. Where you are, what you're looking at.
L1The beat60–90 seconds. The one story, told well, standing alone. Mandatory.
L2The threadThe fuller story it belongs to, running across other stops.
L3The evidenceThe sources, the archive images, the recorded voices. One tap to proof.

5 · The engine underneath

The library isn't decoration — it's the machine. One join turns a flat list of plaques into a web the app can walk in any direction.

A placeStopThe Globe, the Town Hall, the grave…
×
A storyThreadThe Corporation, the port, the butchers…
=
The unit of contentBeatOne story, at one place. The thing you hear and collect.

Why it matters: one place carries many stories, and one story runs through many places — so the Town Hall appears in the Corporation thread and the market-town thread, and "the town that kept its government" plays across four stops. Completing a thread means walking the town, not ticking a list.

The publish gate is built into the machine. A beat cannot go live while any claim behind it is unverified. Nothing a visitor hears is unsourced, and the guardrails (no "Dylan drank at the Globe", no "13th-century cellars", nothing about living people or Corporation finances without consent) are enforced by the software, not by memory.

6 · The heartbeat — the Common Walk

The town beats its bounds every three years. We interpret and host it; we never sell it as a walk — it's a working civic ritual on private ground. In the app, its rarity becomes the feature: one thread that changes state through a three-year cycle, and gives locals a reason to keep the app.

DormantThe whole story readable, past & future tense. "The town next walks its bounds in ___." Never a locked door.
CountdownAs the date nears, the thread wakes. "Notify me before the next Walk."
Live · one dayA different app for a single day — cached that morning. Follow the bounds from home; the Globe is the download point.
AfterglowThe day's voices and photos fold back into the record, and the countdown to the next one begins.

Open question with a real deadline: which year is the next Walk? Whit Monday 2026 has passed; 2027 → 17 May, 2028 → 5 June. If it's 2027 or 2028, filming the real thing becomes a live workstream now. Nothing about the Corporation or the Walk publishes until the Corporation blesses it as a body.
