## 2. FIVE-LINE FINDING

1. **A new named Island House occupant.** Percy Llewellyn Garbett (b. Middlesex, 10 Dec 1852; d. 1924), who took the name **Hughes-Garbett**, was resident at Island House c.1890–1900 — a late-Victorian resident the register did not hold. An insurance manager (New Zealand Insurance, Calcutta; later Norwich Union), otherwise resident away from Laugharne, at the house for a decade.
2. **He reached the house through the Abra/Skyrme/Hughes line.** He **inherited the Laugharne estate in 1885** from **Mary Abra Hughes Skyrme (Wienholt)** — whose name-forms carry the *Abra* forename (consistent with Doc 175's forename finding, not a surname), *Skyrme* (Docs 177/179/191) and *Hughes* — and prepended "Hughes" to Garbett. Her personal estate was £1,450 2s; executor Montague George Hubert Garbett (his brother).
3. **The residence rests on owner-transcribed press.** The site quotes *The Welshman*, Jan 1890, "CHRISTMAS TREE AT ISLAND HOUSE," Mrs Hughes-Garbett hosting the tenants' families — contiguous within one item and naming Island House. But I read the owner's transcription, not the WNO object, so it is Referenced-pending, not Verified.
4. **The 1900 estate auction is corroborated at owner level.** The whole Laugharne estate, Island House included, was sold by auction at **Brown's Hotel, August 1900** (owner-stated proceeds ~£25,000) — corroborating the OPEN "1900-auction" thread. Brown's is the auction **venue** only; no IH–Brown's association is implied.
5. **The site as a restoration record.** Island House Restoration Ltd's own stated objective is to restore the house and convert it to a hotel, bar and restaurant (consistent with SAVE, Docs 185/205); and it object-confirms a **2020 DAT/Heneb excavation** (Trenches 1 & 2), interim reports dated 25 Jan 2021 — the grey-lit object to read next for datable finds.
