## 0. The five-line finding

1. A Laugharne Railway bill was deposited in **November 1865** for the session 1865-6, for a line **from the South Wales Railway near Saint Clears Station to Laugharne**.
2. It became **the Laugharne Railway Act, 1866** — authorising **5 miles 3 furlongs and 9 chains**, commencing in the parish of **Llanfihangel Abercywyn** at a junction with the South Wales Railway.
3. The company came back in **November 1866** for a second Act (session 1867) seeking an **extension to Pendine**, a deviation, abandonment of part of the authorised line, and more capital — and that is the high-water mark of the scheme.
4. By **May 1869** the man who had lent the **£3,360** parliamentary deposit, **Albert Ricardo**, was applying to the Board of Trade to get it back; through **1870** the company was **wound up in Chancery under the Abandonment of Railways Act, 1869**.
5. **The railway was authorised and never opened.** Laugharne got an Act of Parliament for a railway, and never got the railway.

## 2. The objects, read at object level

### 2.1 *London Gazette*, issue 23044, p.6021 — 28 November 1865

Contiguous, within one item:

> "In Parliament.—Session 1865-6. Laugharne Railway. (Incorporation of Company; Power to make Railway from the South Wales Railway near to Saint Clears Station to Laugharne, in the county of Carmarthen; Powers to embank and recla[im]…"

Note the tail: **powers to embank and reclaim**. Laugharne's railway scheme was, in part, a land-reclamation scheme over the estuary flats. That is a thread of its own and is left open below.

### 2.2 *London Gazette*, issue 23191, pp.6494–6495 — 27 November 1866

p.6494, contiguous:

> "In Parliament—Session 1867 … Laugharne Railway (Extension to Pendine; Deviation and Abandonment of portion of Authorised Railway; Increase of Capital; Power to …"

p.6495, contiguous — **and this is the page that settles the Act year**:

> "…provisions, or some of the powers and provisions of the said **Laugharne Railway Act, 1866**, to the said new or substituted Railway. To embank, enclose, drain, reclaim, and improve…"

### 2.3 *London Gazette*, issue 23497, p.2828 — 14 May 1869 — the corrupt object

> "…[A]lbert Ricardo, who lent the amount of the deposit of £3360, referred to in the 26th section of 'The **Laugharne Railway Act, 1856**,' has made application in writing to the Board of Trade…"

### 2.4 *The Welshman*, 21 & 28 May and 4 June 1869, p.4 — the independent witness

The same Board of Trade notice, advertised locally three weeks running. A **different corpus, different scan, different typesetting** — which is exactly what an OCR dispute needs. It prints **1866** each time, and on 4 June it carries the Act's own description of the line:

> "…'The Laugharne Railway Act, 1866,' has made application in writing to the Board of Trade, setting forth that the said Albert Ricardo is desirous that the Railway by the said Act authorized to be made, and therein described as a Railway **5 miles 3 furlongs and 9 chains in length, commencing in the parish of Llanfihangel-Abercowin, in the County of Carmarthen, by a junction with the South Wales** [Railway]…"

### 2.5 *London Gazette*, issues 23617 / 23625 / 23631 / 23643 — the end, 1870

- **23617, p.2661 (20 May 1870):** "…all persons having any claims or demands upon the said Laugharne Railway Company for compensation or otherwise, **by reason of the abandonment** of the hereinbefore-mentioned rail[way]…"
- **23625, p.3013 (17 June 1870):** the petition set down before **Vice-Chancellor Sir William Milbourne James** for 25 June 1870.
- **23631, p.3281 (5 July 1870):** "In the Matter of the Companies Acts, 1862 and 1867, and of the **Abandonment of Railways Act, 1869**, and in the Matter of the Laugharne Railway Company. BY an Order made by the Vice-Chancellor Sir William Milbourne James…"
- **23643, p.3797 (12 August 1870):** a further order of **Vice-Chancellor Sir James Bacon**, dated 5 August 1870.

### 2.6 Coflein / RCAHMW — a negative, logged as a negative

A search of the National Monuments Record of Wales for "Laugharne railway" returns **one** site and it is irrelevant (NPRN 266283, Haroldston House garden earthworks, Haverfordwest). RCAHMW holds no site record for a railway at Laugharne. **Supporting, not probative** — absence from a national record is not proof of absence on the ground, and this was one spelling only.

## 3. Settling the Act year

| Printing | Year given |
|---|---|
| *London Gazette* 23191, p.6495 (27 Nov 1866) | **1866** |
| *The Welshman*, 21 May 1869, p.4 | **1866** |
| *The Welshman*, 28 May 1869, p.4 | **1866** |
| *The Welshman*, 4 Jun 1869, p.4 | **1866** |
| *London Gazette* 23497, p.2828 (14 May 1869) | 1856 |

Four to one, across two independent corpora — and **1856 is impossible on the face of the record anyway**, because the incorporating bill was not deposited until November 1865. Tiered **Verified-strong**.

**Do not print a regnal citation.** The Act is almost certainly 29 & 30 Vict., but that has not been read and will not be asserted.

## 4. THE METHOD FINDING — snippet-shifting

This is the part of the run worth keeping.

### 4.1 The page-level trap is not peculiar to Welsh Newspapers Online

Doc 141 established that WNO's AND matches at **page** level, not sentence level, and that a genuine-looking hit can rest on two towns sharing a page. That trap produced the false "High Street, Laugharne" finding.

**The Gazette behaves identically.** Twice in this run:

- `"Laugharne Railway" + "St. Clears"` returned Gazette 23497 p.2828 — a page whose leading item is a petition concerning the **Commercial Bank Corporation of India and the East**.
- `"Laugharne Railway" + warrant + abandonment` returned 23617 p.2661 — a page whose leading item concerns **cotton spinners of Blackburn**.

Neither is evidence of anything. **The rule generalises: co-occurrence on a page is not evidence; evidence is contiguity within one item.** It should now be assumed true of every page-scanned corpus this desk touches until shown otherwise.

### 4.2 Snippet-shifting — the technique that defeats it

A search engine that returns a **keyword-centred snippet** can be walked along a single item, because the window moves to wherever the match is. Search a phrase you can see at the *end* of your current snippet, and the engine re-centres and hands you the next stretch of the same item.

Worked example. The Gazette snippet for the 1865 notice died at:

> "…Power to make Railway from the South Wales Railway near to Sai…"

Re-searching the exact phrase `"Power to make Railway from the South Wales Railway"` re-centred the window and returned:

> "…Power to make Railway from the South Wales Railway **near to Saint Clears Station to Laugharne, in the county of Carmarthen; Powers to embank and recla…**"

The same technique on WNO recovered the Act's mileage and commencing parish out of the middle of a 6,293-word advertising column.

**Why this matters beyond this thread:** it converts a page-level search engine into an item-level reader *without* opening the facsimile. It does not merely avoid trap 2 — it **defeats** it, because the contiguity is demonstrated by the engine itself. It is reusable on The Gazette, on WNO, and on any snippet-returning corpus, and it should be the default move before any negative is recorded.

**Its limit:** it reads OCR, not the page. Sources gathered this way are marked **Read = Partial**, never "Read". Where the OCR is corrupt, snippet-shifting will faithfully hand you the corruption — which is why F2 needed a second corpus, not a second search.

## 5. What must NOT be written

- **Not "the railway that never came" as a Dylan line.** No Dylan framing attaches to this at all. It is the town's own record and stands on its own.
- **Not "not a sod was turned" / "not a spade went into the ground."** *Never opened* is proved. *Never begun* is not — abandonment under the 1869 Act is perfectly compatible with earthworks having been started. Tiered **Verified**, deliberately not Verified-strong.
- **Not "Pendine was authorised."** November 1866 is a **notice of intention to apply**. Whether the 1867 Act passed is unestablished.
- **Not a regnal citation for the Act.**
- **Not "Albert Ricardo the economist's family."** The identity of Albert Ricardo is entirely unestablished. He is the lender of a deposit and nothing more until someone reads a source.

Safe public wording: **"In 1866 Laugharne got an Act of Parliament for its own railway — five and a half miles from the main line near St Clears. The company spent four years failing to build it, and in 1870 the Court of Chancery wound it up. The line never opened."**
