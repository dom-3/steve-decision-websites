### Five-line finding

1. **Cadw 9670 read at object level** via the British Listed Buildings mirror `300009670`, which reproduces the Cadw list text (the canonical Cadw FullReport endpoint was egress-blocked to automated fetch this pass — the same tool-level class recorded at Docs 188/194/196). Grade II, listed **25 September 1986**, Building Class Commercial, listed for **GROUP VALUE only** — the honest tier is contextual townscape value, not individual merit, exactly as for the Globe, Brown's and the Ship & Castle.
2. **Fabric:** early C19, **shown on the 1834 survey map**; late Georgian, 3-storey 3-window roughcast front with plinth and rusticated quoins, slate roof and roughcast end stacks, 6- and 12-pane sash windows with lugged architraves, and an ornate bracketed hood over the central entrance with pilasters and a 6-panel door. A lower 2-storey range adjoins forward to the street on the left, carrying a **low segmental coaching entrance with double doors**; the right gable end faces Market Street. The rear cross range is "not of special interest."
3. **Location & identity:** at the crossroads of Market Street, King Street and Newbridge Street (OS SN302108); also recorded as the **New Three Mariners Inn** (Wikidata Q29488427).
4. **Coflein independence test = null on text:** the RCAHMW record **NPRN 17602** is a bare stub ("No description available"). It gives no independent descriptive corroboration of the Cadw listing — the same outcome as the Island House main-house (NPRN 17457, Doc 187) and garage (NPRN 31614, Doc 189) stubs — but it independently confirms the site as a **PUBLIC HOUSE, Post Medieval**, at grid **SN3020010880**, a datum in agreement with Cadw.
5. **One archive object + a guarded address variant:** NPRN 17602 carries a single Related Archive — **CLBN negative 6545144 / CLBN_WRH_019_19**, a 35mm colour negative of the inn by **William R. Hughes for Cadw, December 1985**, undigitised. Its caption gives the address as **"Victoria Street"**, against Cadw's Market/King/Newbridge crossroads. Victoria Street is a real adjacent street in this cluster (Brown's is described as near "the crossroads with Victoria Street"), so the variant is **logged, not reconciled by assumption** (near-collision discipline, cf. "Logan Street" Doc 187, "Llaugharne" Doc 189).

### The object, in full (Cadw 9670, exterior)

> Early C19 (shown on 1834 survey map) late Georgian 3-storey, 3-window roughcast front with plinth and rusticated quoins; slate roof, roughcast end chimney stacks. 6 and 12-pane sash windows with lugged architraves; ornate bracketed hood to central entrance with pilasters and 6-panel door. Lower 2-storey roughcast range adjoins forward to the street on left, similar detail but replaced sash glazing, low segmental coaching entrance with double doors. Right gable end to Market Street (E side) has similar detail to the front. Cross range to rear is not of special interest.

Reason for listing: **Group value.**

The **coaching entrance** is Verified *fabric* from the listing. Whether the inn *operated* as a coaching inn is an inference the record does not make — it is held as an open lead, not asserted. Likewise the name **"New Three Mariners"** implies a predecessor "Three Mariners" establishment; that is a reasonable inference but unconfirmed by any object read this pass, so it is recorded as an open lead only, not a fact.

- **Details:** Type PUBLIC HOUSE, Period Post Medieval, Community Laugharne Township, Map ref SN31SW, Grid Reference SN3020010880. Description field: *"No description available."*
- **Related Archives (1):** archive no. **6545144**, title **CLBN_WRH_019_19 — Cadw Listed Buildings 35mm Colour Negative Collection**, Item level, described as a "Colour negative showing New Three Mariner's Inn, Victoria Street, Laugharne, produced by William R. Hughes, Cadw, December 1985." Medium: Photo. Not digitised.

The record therefore corroborates the Cadw listing only at the level of **site existence and grid datum** — not fabric, not dating. This is a genuine independence result (null on descriptive text), recorded honestly. The negative slots into the same 1985–86 W.R. Hughes CLBN survey run as Doc 187's ten Island House main-house negatives and Doc 189's garden negative (6545210) — an archive-day order target from RCAHMW.
