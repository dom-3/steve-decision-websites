## 0. One-line finding

The Manse is the broader, stuccoed half of a **late-C18/early-C19 Georgian pair** — "Manse and Pelican" — listed Grade II under one record (Cadw 9640, 25 September 1986, for group value) on the crossroads frontage opposite Brown's Hotel; the object read cleanly at object level from Cadw's Full Report and is corroborated (identity, type and pairing) by Coflein NPRN 17552, which additionally places the house on **King Street** with its "back garden leads down to the Coran." Two payloads sit inside the object and both are earned by it: a **2nd-floor plaque on Pelican naming "W and M Davies" and a build of 1866** (held Referenced-pending — the Cadw transcription is partly illegible, and 1866 attaches to the plaque, not to the Georgian frontages), and — stated by the **statutory record itself** — that Pelican "was from 1948 the home of Dylan Thomas' parents." That last is a genuine, object-sourced Thomas-**family** residence; it is handled as such, at tier, with the guardrail applied.

## 1. Why this target

Doc 224's next-target named the Manse (Cadw 9640) explicitly as "the cleanest desk-runnable in-lane object," and the desk has been building the town's crossroads frontage steadily — the Town Hall (Doc 217), Gwalia & Manchester House (Doc 218), Great House on King Street (Doc 202), the New Three Mariners (Doc 204), Castle House (Doc 210). The Manse fills a gap in that block: it sits "opposite Brown's Hotel," attached to Corran Books (the former Ship & Castle) to its right, near the Victoria Street crossroads. It is a single discrete object that rewards depth, which suits the one-target rule; it is not an Island-House target (Island House is the parallel branch's queue and carries no overlap here). The register had **no** prior row for the Manse or Cadw 9640 — this is genuinely un-done.

## 2. The object, read direct

Primary read at object level, 23 July 2026, from Cadw's own Full Report for Listed Buildings, id 9640, and cross-checked against the British Listed Buildings transcription (300009640, verbatim-identical, therefore **not** an independent authority). An independent national-monuments record — Coflein/RCAHMW NPRN 17552 — was then read direct.

**Cadw Full Report 9640 (primary):**

- **Designation:** Grade II, designated 25 September 1986. Reference 9640. Broad class: Domestic. **Group description: "Manse and Pelican."** Reason for designation: Group value.
- **Location:** "Opposite Brown's Hotel." Easting 230188, Northing 210917 (≈ SN 3018 1091; lat 51.7711, long −4.4625).
- **Exterior (verbatim, incl. Cadw's own ellipsis in the plaque quote):** "Late C18/early C19 Georgian pair of 3-storey and basement, 3-window frontages, broader and stuccoed to Manse, cement render to Pelican; the latter was from 1948 the home of Dylan Thomas' parents. Slate roof, brick chimney stacks, eaves band. Recessed small-pane sash windows, offset to right, with central Palladian semi-circular headed sashes with keystones. Bracketed hoods to entrances; 6-panel door to Manse, 9-panel door to Pelican with fanlights. Plaque to 2nd floor of Pelican reads \"The two .... pine ends of this building belong to W and M Davies. **Coflein/RCAHMW NPRN 17552 (secondary), "Manse, King Street, Laugharne," HOUSE, Post Medieval, compiled John Wiles 22.11.07:**

- Grid reference SN3018510915, map ref SN31SW.
- **Description (verbatim):** "With the Pelican (NPRN 17646) one of a pair of late eighteenth-early nineteenth century Georgian houses. This is a three storey building with basement(?) under a slate gabled roof with brick end chimney stacks. The stuccoed three bay street front rises to an eaves band and there is a central doorway under a bracketed hood. The ground floor and first floor windows are twelve pane sashes with a fine Palladian round headed window with keystones above the doorway. The upper storey six pane windows are near square. There is a rear service range. **The back garden leads down to the Coran.**"
- **Cited source:** "CADW Listed Buildings Database (9640)." So NPRN 17552 is **not independent of Cadw in authority** — but it adds the King Street address, the garden-to-Corran topography, the 12-pane sashes and a rear service range as the compiler's own site observations. Sibling record: Pelican, NPRN 17646 (carries 2 images).

## 3. What the object earns — facts and tiers

**Fact 1 — VERIFIED.** The Manse and Pelican are a late-C18/early-C19 Georgian pair (3-storey with basement, 3-window frontages; the Manse broader and stuccoed, Pelican cement-rendered), listed Grade II on 25 September 1986 for group value (Cadw 9640). Straight off the designation record. Single Cadw authority (BLB is a verbatim transcription, not independent), so **Verified, not Verified-strong.**

**Fact 2 — VERIFIED.** Each frontage carries a central **Palladian semi-circular-headed sash with keystones** above the doorway, under bracketed entrance hoods; the Manse has a 6-panel door, Pelican a 9-panel door with fanlights; slate roof, brick chimney stacks, eaves band. Cadw's exterior text, with the Palladian keystoned window and stuccoed three-bay front **independently described** by Coflein 17552 (which adds 12-pane ground/first-floor sashes and near-square upper-storey 6-pane windows). This architectural detail is the pair's signature on the street.

**Fact 3 — VERIFIED (as to what the record states).** The Cadw statutory listing **itself** records that Pelican "was from 1948 the home of **Dylan Thomas' parents**." This is an object-level, town-fabric attestation of a Thomas-**family** residence — the parents', not the poet's. It is Verified only as to what the Cadw record asserts. The wider Dylan literature commonly dates the parents' move to Pelican to **1949**; Cadw says 1948. That one-year tension is **flagged and left unreconciled** (independent biography not yet read at object level). See guardrail §5.

**Fact 4 — REFERENCED-PENDING.** A 2nd-floor plaque on Pelican names "**W and M Davies**" and records a build of **1866** ("Built by him in 1866"). Held Referenced-pending for two honest reasons: (i) Cadw's own transcription is **partly illegible** — it prints "The two **....** pine ends of this building belong to W and M Davies," so the exact wording is uncertain; and (ii) **1866 attaches to the plaque / a partial rebuild, not to the dating of the Georgian frontages**, which the same record dates late-C18/early-C19. The 1866 date must not be read as the build date of the house.

**Fact 5 — VERIFIED.** Coflein/RCAHMW (NPRN 17552) places the Manse on **King Street** and records that its "back garden leads down to the Coran" (the Corran brook/creek), with a rear service range. NPRN 17552 cites Cadw 9640 as its source and so is not independent in authority, but the King Street address and the garden-to-Corran topography are **Coflein's own additions** (absent from the Cadw text) and are Verified on that basis. King Street ties the Manse into the crossroads frontage block already mapped at Docs 202 (Great House), 204 (New Three Mariners), 210 (Castle House) and 218 (Gwalia & Manchester House).

## 5. Guardrails observed

- **The Dylan handling.** This is the delicate one. The banned shape is *"his pub was Brown's, BUT here's our Dylan link"* — a structure that demands a Dylan connection exist and regenerates the retracted Globe/Beynon error with a new noun. That is **not** what is happening here: the Pelican–Thomas fact is **stated by the statutory record itself**, is about Dylan's **parents' home** (not Dylan's, not a drinking place), and is recorded at tier without being spun into a poet's-footsteps hook. Reading the object honestly *includes* reading that sentence; suppressing it would be as much a distortion as inflating it. The claim is fenced: parents' residence, Cadw's word "from 1948" (with the 1949 tension flagged), no assertion that Dylan lived, wrote or drank there.
- **No mis-dating from the plaque.** 1866 is the plaque's date and belongs to a partial rebuild / the Davies gable-ends; it is **not** promoted to the build date of the Georgian frontages.
- **No invented antiquity.** The house is late-C18/early-C19 on the record; it is not called medieval or older.
- **Corporation / Globe wording untouched.** No Globe claim is made or reconciled here (the Globe's own High-street/Duncan-Street question stays open, Doc 167b).
- **Publish gate.** Nothing here is proposed for the public trail; tiering only. Doc left uncommitted for Jarvis's 21:00 run.

## 6. Open routes (recorded, not walked)

1. **The 1949 vs 1948 Thomas-at-Pelican date.** Read a Dylan biography / the Dylan Thomas Boathouse or literary-trust record at object level to settle whether the parents moved to Pelican in 1948 (Cadw) or 1949 (commonly cited), and to fix exactly which parent(s) and dates — moving Fact 3's biographical layer off Referenced-pending. Keep strictly to the parents' residence; do not let it become a Dylan-site claim.
2. **W. and M. Davies and the 1866 plaque.** The 1901/1911 census, Kelly's/Slater's directories, and a full on-site read of the plaque (the "...." gap) could give forenames, trade and the meaning of "the two ... ends" — and confirm whether 1866 marks a rebuild of Pelican's gable-ends.
3. **Pelican's own Cadw record and NPRN 17646.** Pelican is listed in its own right (separate BLB "Pelican" entry) and has its own Coflein NPRN 17646 with two images — read both for any independent fabric/occupancy detail and a possible second authority (would test Facts 1/2 toward Verified-strong).
4. **The crossroads frontage sweep.** A trade-directory pass (Pigot 1830/44, Slater's, Kelly's 1884/95) across the Manse–Corran Books–Brown's–New Three Mariners block to date the conversions and occupiers — trap-prone under automation (WNO page-level / near-collision), flagged for the human archive day.
