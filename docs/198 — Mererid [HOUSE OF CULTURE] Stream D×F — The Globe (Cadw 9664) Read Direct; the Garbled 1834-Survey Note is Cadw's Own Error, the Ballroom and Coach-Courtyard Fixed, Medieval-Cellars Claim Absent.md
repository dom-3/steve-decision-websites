Doc 132 is the verified Globe *story*; Doc 136 is the 1830 newspaper advert. Nothing had put the Cadw list description on the record and tiered it. ## The object, verbatim where it matters

The Cadw record is short. The full body of the statutory text:

- **Name of property:** "Globe House (including SB & RB Gleed, Butcher's Shop)"
- **Location:** "Opposite the town Hall at the end of the street, on the corner with Duncan Street. Formerly the Globe Hotel, sold in 1869."
- **History:** "Mid C19 (now show on 1834 survey map) late Georgian double pile plan."
- **Exterior:** "3-storey and basement 3-window rendered front with 2nd floor cill band. Slate roof, projecting eaves, brick and rubble chimney stacks. 2-storey bay to left … Wide bracketed hood to doorways of house and shop; 6-panel door to house, boarded stable door to shop with later bay window. … Cross ran[g]e extends along Duncan Street (N side) in which cambered archway with keystone and double boarded doors lead to rectangular cobbled courtyard with whitewashed rubble inner walls."
- **Interior:** "Interior retains contemporary detail including Regency fireplaces, one with paired columns; shutters, cornices and panelled doors. Narrow ballroom to cross range."
- **Reason for designation:** "Group value."

## Findings

**1. Designation.** Grade II, designated 25 September 1986, ref 9664, listed for **group value** — with the adjoining Gwalia and Manchester House, opposite the Grade II\* Town Hall. It is not listed for exceptional individual merit. *(Verified-strong.)*

**2. The Gleed butcher's shop is in the property name.** The statutory record names the property "Globe House (including SB & RB Gleed, Butcher's Shop)" — the listing itself carries the Gleed shop, which retains a boarded stable door with a later bay window. This is an object-level anchor for the Gleeds at the Globe. *(Verified-strong.)* Guardrail held: the Gleeds were butchers at the Globe c.1911–1991 — around **eighty years**, never "a century", never "the town's last butcher".

**3. "Formerly the Globe Hotel, sold in 1869."** The former-hotel status and the 1869 sale are stated in the Location field of the record itself. *(Verified.)* The sale-notice particulars — lot description, price, purchaser — are **not** in the listing and remain a separate open thread (`recPlA1qLpfSKgCEZ`).

**4. The garbled "1834 survey" note is Cadw's own error, not a transcription artefact.** The History field reads, verbatim, **"Mid C19 (now show on 1834 survey map) late Georgian double pile plan."** The malformed parenthetical is **identical** in the Cadw FullReport (primary) and in British Listed Buildings — so it is an error in Cadw's own record and cannot be corrected from the online text alone. On internal logic, a mid-C19 build post-dates the 1834 survey, so the intended sense is almost certainly **"not shown on the 1834 survey map"**. Held at **Referenced-pending**: definitive correction needs the paper list or the 1986 BSAHI Laugharne survey (archive day). Per Doc 164b, that 1834 survey is E. Beddoe Hughes's *Survey of Corporation Lands*, so absence from it proves nothing unless the Globe stood on Corporation land — the dating rests on a weak foundation either way.

**5. The standing house post-dates the establishment.** Cadw dates the visible fabric "Mid C19 … late Georgian double pile plan" — a 3-storey-and-basement, 3-window rendered, double-pile late-Georgian house. But Doc 136 (the 1830 advert) places an operating Globe Inn under Thomas Edwards by 1830, with assembly balls by 1835. The **establishment predates the current fabric**; the standing house is best read as a rebuild or refronting of an earlier inn. The mid-C19 date is Cadw's stylistic assessment, not a documentary one. *(Verified as Cadw's dating; tension logged.)*

**6. The coach-house/courtyard is object-confirmed (unblocks the coach-house thread).** A cross range extends along Duncan Street (N side) with a **cambered archway with keystone and double boarded doors** leading to a **rectangular cobbled courtyard with whitewashed rubble inner walls** — the carriage entry and yard of the former coaching inn. *(Verified.)*

**7. The ballroom is object-confirmed and located (unblocks the assembly-room thread).** The interior retains a **"narrow ballroom to cross range"**, plus Regency fireplaces (one with paired columns), shutters, cornices and panelled doors. The ballroom sits in the cross range **along Duncan Street**; the Regency detail is consistent with an early-C19 fit-out, matching the documented assembly balls by 1835. *(Verified.)*

**8. The medieval-cellars claim is absent from the statutory record (bears on the cellars thread).** The Cadw text describes only a **"basement"**. It does **not** call the cellars barrel-vaulted, and it does **not** date them medieval or 13th-century. The popular "four medieval barrel-vaulted 13th-century cellars" account is not in the listing; it rests on secondary sources (Laugharne Lines). Down-tiered to **Referenced-pending**. **Guardrail:** on the public trail the cellars are "atmospheric barrel-vaulted" — **NEVER "13th-century"**. The fabric-date question belongs to a physical survey; the cellars thread (`recXHzVPqSIXNp6ax`) stays open on that point.

## Chain of custody
