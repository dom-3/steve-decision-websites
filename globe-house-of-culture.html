The Globe · House of Culture

The living heart of Laugharne

The cultural & experience layer of The Globe — turning the ~15,000 Dylan Thomas Boathouse visitors a year (plus the castle, the coast path and the Under Milk Wood pilgrims) into a run-it-ourselves, whole-day, whole-town experience. Not a Boathouse alternative — the warm, well-run day the visitor actually hoped for.

The Boathouse is the shrine. The Globe is the living heart — where the story of Dylan, of Laugharne and of the Globe itself is told, experienced, eaten and taken home.

The opportunity, in one line

What the reviews tell us — and the gap we fill.

Visitors love the views, the staff and the Welsh cakes — but the Boathouse exhibition and video feel dated, the path is steep with no parking, the price feels steep "for the size," and reliable evening dining is thin. It's a 20-minute stop crying out to be a day. We turn a short pilgrimage into a full-day experience that starts and ends at the Globe — and lifts the whole town with it.

The app — built for the tech-useless

Dead-simple flow.
"Go to this page. Start here. Do this. Here's the bit you're at. Listen to this. Look out for this." One tap to the next stop. Audio narration, spot-it games and prompts along the way. Downloadable / offline for the dead-signal stretches on the coast path. Ends at coffee, cake, food — and book dinner.
Fed by verified history.
Every stop's story pulls from Mererid's sourced facts — nothing overclaimed. The town-wide culture database (Laugharne + Island House + Boathouse + Castle) becomes the content pipeline behind a working app, not a launch blocker.

Tiered trails

One map, many journeys — accessible loops through to long walks, each with history at every stop.

Easy · accessibleThe Town Loop — short, flat, buggy- and wheelchair-friendly. The Globe, the square, the Under Milk Wood corners. The answer to "steep path, no parking."
MediumThe Dylan Loop — Globe → Boathouse → writing shed → castle → back. The classic pilgrimage, done properly and bookended by us.
Longer · harderThe Coast & Estuary Trail — the walk Dylan walked, the "heron-priested shore." For the pilgrims who want the whole day on foot.

The town's app, the Globe's bookend — built as a cultural asset for the whole of Laugharne to use (opens Heritage Fund and awards eligibility), with every journey quietly starting and finishing at the Globe.✓
Mererid runs continuously — automated research every 30 minutes off a thread backlog; one verified thread per run, logged to the chain of custody.✓
Cadw — approach with a demo, not a deck — they're award-recognised for immersive heritage storytelling (M+H Awards 2026), so we go to them from strength once the app is real.✓
Verified or it doesn't publish — Mererid's register is an enforced gate in the app itself: a beat cannot go live while any linked claim is unverified, and every published sentence traces to its source in one click.✓
The Globe earns the bookend — Stop 0 carries zero commercial content; we hold both ends by being useful (wifi & download point, loo, water, charge, map, the place a split party reunites). Test for every line: "would this still be here if the Globe didn't own the app?"✓
Dylan's recorded voice is off the table — sound recordings carry a separate term plus performers' rights, so text entering the public domain does not release the archive audio.✓
Professional commissioned narration — our own prose, our own voice, rights-proof from day one. Copyright comes off the critical path: Mererid's verification now only decides whether we may quote Dylan, not whether the app works.✓
Bilingual, properly — Welsh and English built in from the start, not retrofitted. Scripts, content model and audio pipeline all bilingual by design.✓
Self-funded, done properly — no grant applications or funding vehicles pursued at this stage. We build it to a professional standard on our own terms; awards can be revisited once it's real.✓
Dylan's voice — Laugharne area only — in the UK his words entered the public domain in Jan 2024 and pre-1962 sound recordings were never revived by the 2013 term extension, so the 1952–53 Caedmon recordings appear clear on all three rights layers. We restrict to the Laugharne area — a strict subset of that position rather than a test of it. Karen leads compliance.✓
AI narration, no voice budget — AI carries the narration spine (re-records are free, so versioning costs almost nothing), Welsh comes from local speakers as a parallel script, and the town's own recorded voices become the authentic layer nobody can copy. Gated on an accent test first.✓
Never synthesise Dylan's voice — we use the real licensed recordings or we use our own narration. No AI approximation of a real or deceased person, ever. It would be an ethical and rights failure, and it would poison the credibility the whole project is built on.✓
If it isn't in the index, it doesn't exist — every document logged to Docs — Index in the shared base with its exact location, the moment it's written. Two handoffs have already degraded because a persona couldn't find another's work.✓
Self-hosted Welsh voice (Bangor / Techiaith) — open-source bilingual Welsh–English voices built from a native-speaker corpus, self-hostable. Bilingual by construction, so its English is already Welsh-accented — our requirement, not a workaround. Local speaker recorded once, then every beat and every future correction generated free, in a real accent.✓
We do not sell a neighbour's voice — some providers take a perpetual licence to build derivatives from uploaded voices. We will never trade a Laugharne neighbour's voice likeness to a company in perpetuity for a free tier. Any provider demanding it is disqualified; the model stays ours and theirs.✓
Town voices are v1, not a nice-to-have — with a synthetic spine, the real voices are what stop this sounding like every other heritage app, and they're the one asset nobody can copy. Waiting has an irreversible cost.✓
