## 1. Five-line finding

1. **There is exactly ONE Skyrme of Laugharne in the entire NLW Welsh probate index, and he is far too early.** All seventeen exact-`Skyrme` records (1556–1858) were read at object level across both result pages. The sole Laugharne entry: *"Skyrme, William, Laugharne, Laugharne, Carmarthen, Esq; St. David's Probate Records, 1556-1858"*, will **1806**, reference **SD1806-70**.

2. **A SECOND William Skyrme of Laugharne exists, and the programme had never seen him** — will proved at the **Prerogative Court of Canterbury, 27 August 1823, TNA PROB 11/1674/373**, register *Richards*, quires 451–500. He is wholly absent from the NLW index, which is why seventeen prior probate passes missed him.

3. **The F177-3 route is REFUTED as framed.** Doc 177 predicted a will "proved c.1830–1858" carrying an heiress clause. Both jurisdictions covering that window were searched exhaustively and **both are empty** for Laugharne Skyrmes. The only candidates are 1806 and 1823. The date window is refuted; the thread is not.

4. **The Hughes name was already inside the Skyrme family by 1806** — *"Will of Francis otherwise Francis Hughes Skyrme of Vaynor, Pembrokeshire"*, **PCC PROB 11/1450/326**, 20 November 1806. That is precisely the name-carrying mechanism the thread's own candidate list predicts, attested half a century early. It is a lead of the right shape and **is not** an answer.

5. **Two method results, both of which change how the desk works.** PCC and NLW diocesan probate are *complementary* indexes, not duplicates — proved here by two men of one town and one surname appearing in one index each, with no overlap. And the NLW Primo probate interface, which Doc 177 declared unreadable under automation, **reads perfectly well**; that warning was true of a tool path and false of the site.

## 3. What this settles, and what it does not

### 3.1 Settled

- Two distinct Laugharne Skyrme probates exist, seventeen years and two jurisdictions apart: **1806 (NLW SD1806-70)** and **1823 (PCC PROB 11/1674/373)**.
- No Laugharne Skyrme will exists in either index between 1823 and 1858.
- The Skyrme name has a Pembrokeshire seat at **Vaynor, Llawhaden**, `Esq`-ranked across four probates from 1713 to 1806.

### 3.2 Not settled — and not to be smoothed over

**Which William is "the late William Skyrme, Esq., of Laugharne" of 1858?** Three readings remain live and untested:

- **(a) The 1823 man, dead thirty-five years.** Doc 177 evidenced a Skyrme daughter born 1813, with Laugharne ringing its bells. A woman born 1813 whose father died by 1823 would be about forty-five in 1858 and would correctly be styled daughter of *the late* William Skyrme. This coheres well. **It is a hypothesis and must not be narrated.**
- **(b) He died intestate**, and there is no will to find.
- **(c) He died during 1858 itself**, after probate passed to the civil Principal Probate Registry in January of that year. That calendar sits behind a paywall and is therefore archive-gated for this desk.

**The Hughes–Skyrme mechanism is still unknown.** F179-4 is tiered Referenced-pending precisely because the temptation to close F177-3 with it is strong and wrong. Nothing read joins the Vaynor Francis to the Laugharne Williams, and the "otherwise" formula's cause — a name assumed under will or settlement, most likely — is stated nowhere that has been read. The will body would state it. It has not been read.

> **The standing prohibition is unchanged and is repeated here: do not write her as "Abra Skyrme" or "née Hughes" anywhere.**

### 3.3 The gap, stated plainly

As with Bridget Bevan (Doc 173) and the Skyrme family generally (Doc 177): **no source read this pass places any Skyrme at Island House, or at any named Laugharne house.** The Skyrme–Island House attachment remains **UNSOURCED**. Reporting that is the finding, not a failure.

## 4. Method results

### 4.1 PCC and NLW are complementary — F179-5

Every probate pass this programme has run (Docs 111, 114, 175, 177) searched the NLW diocesan index alone. Diocesan jurisdiction stops at the diocesan boundary: a testator whose goods lay in more than one diocese proved at the Prerogative Court of Canterbury instead, and is therefore *absent from NLW altogether*.

Demonstrated here, not asserted: the 1823 Laugharne Skyrme is in PCC and not NLW; the 1806 Laugharne Skyrme is in NLW and not PCC. **Two men, one town, one surname, two indexes, no overlap.**

> **Consequence for the whole desk: every "no will survives" negative recorded against the NLW index alone is UNSAFE and must be re-run against PROB 11 before it can stand.**
> Named exposures: **Pennoyre Watkins 1787** (`rec46ijo9UuIUQ5CR`), the **Prosser/Peppler identification**, and the **three St David's wills of Doc 111**.

A side-observation worth keeping: proving at PCC often signals property spanning more than one diocese. For a Laugharne family, that would mean property across the Carmarthen/Pembroke boundary — which is exactly the Haverfordwest–Laugharne double footing Doc 177 evidenced. *Suggestive, not proven.* PCC was also used for reasons of status alone, and this must not be treated as evidence of estate.
