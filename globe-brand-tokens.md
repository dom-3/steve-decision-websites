## Voice — do / don't
**Do:** name suppliers, town, maker · few words · speak of season & tide · be generous & human · let the photo/dish talk.
**Don't:** hype/superlatives/exclamations · corporate or salesy phrasing · invented heritage or unprovable claims · long hedged sentences · branch name without "The Globe".

**Voice in each light:**
- Restaurant — booked, small, exact; the fleeting seasonal dish.
- Bakery — plain and factual, no adjectives. Approved: *"Bread baked in Laugharne, every morning."*
  ⚠️ The ingredient line is ON HOLD pending per-product verification — see the ruling below.

> 🚫 **RULED — compliance sign-off, Karen, 22 Jul 2026. Applies to every property. Not a draft.**
>
> **1. "Organic" is PROHIBITED.** We hold no certificate, and no control body or certification code is recorded anywhere. Baking is *preparation* under the retained organic regulations: a business that prepares and sells product described as organic must itself be registered with an approved control body. **Certification does not travel down the supply chain with the ingredient** — buying certified organic flour does not make our loaf organic. Using it is an offence under the organic regulations and separately a misleading commercial practice under CPUT 2008. Enforced by **Trading Standards**, not the EHO.
> **This includes page metadata** — meta and og descriptions are customer-facing commercial communications and are caught identically. This is the one everybody misses.
>
> **2. "A loaf with nothing bad in it" is PROHIBITED.** A general, non-specific health claim; only permitted alongside a specific authorised claim, and none exists that it could attach to. It also implies other bakers' bread is harmful (fair trading), and it is unsubstantiable in principle — salt is in the loaf.
>
> **3. Approved replacement wording:**
> - H1 — *"Bread baked in Laugharne, every morning."*
> - Spec panel — *"Flour: stoneground, from [mill name]"* — name the mill, make no quality claim.
> - Meta description — *"A small bakery on the estuary at Laugharne. Bread baked fresh each morning, sold the day it's made."*
> - Ingredient line — *"Four ingredients. Flour, water, salt, time."* ⚠️ **Only if the recipe truly is those four. If it contains yeast it is five — verify per product before use.**
>
> **4. "No improvers, no emulsifiers" — ON HOLD, not approved.** Permitted if literally true (factual absence statements aren't health claims), but requires the baker's confirmation **and the mill's flour spec sheet**: commercial flour frequently arrives already containing ascorbic acid (E300), which is an improver. The claim can be false through no fault of the baker.
>
> Owner: Karen. Items 1 and 2 are settled — do not reopen without a certificate. Item 4 releases the moment the baker and mill answer.
